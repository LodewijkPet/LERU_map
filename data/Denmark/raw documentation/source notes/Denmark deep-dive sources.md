# Denmark deep-dive sources

## Starting point from the local dossier

The existing Denmark dossier was already strong on system structure before this pass. The main task here was not to rediscover the system, but to tighten the publication reading.

The local overview and dossier already showed:

- a statutory split between national misconduct handling and institution-level questionable research practice QRP handling
- a national publication route through Nævnet for Videnskabelig Uredelighed NVU
- multiple university practice-committee or responsible-conduct routes
- a code-based integrity layer that now runs through the 2026 Danish Code of Conduct for Research Integrity

What was still unclear was how far the public case trail really goes, how the national and institutional publication layers interact, and whether the English NVU pages are the same archive as the Danish ones.

## Key official source groups checked in this pass

- NVU English board page and current Danish board page
- Danish decisions archive and English decisions page
- NVU Annual Report 2024 on research misconduct
- NVU Annual Report 2024 on institution-reported questionable research practice
- UCPH Practice Committee page, publications page and Annual Report 2024
- Aarhus University committee page and rules for the Research Practice Committee

## Key conclusions for coding

- The strongest national archive is the Danish-language NVU year archive, not the English decisions page.
- The Danish archive carries both current NVU decisions and older predecessor-regime material, so archive continuity has to be coded separately from current body identity.
- Denmark has two national publication layers under the same national owner: direct anonymized misconduct decisions and a separate annual QRP report built from institution reporting.
- Public decisions and annual reports show a referral loop: NVU can close the misconduct question and send possible QRP issues back to the research institution.
- Local institutional publication is real but uneven. UCPH is currently the clearest example because it publishes annual reports and decision minutes with case numbering and summaries.
- Aarhus has a strong route and an explicit annual summary duty in its rules, but no public AU case archive was identified in this pass.

## Committee and case-file pass

### Seed bodies

- Body: Nævnet for Videnskabelig Uredelighed NVU
- Type: National statutory misconduct board
- Lane: Research misconduct FFP
- Role: Final national decision maker on research misconduct and annual publication owner for misconduct reporting and national QRP reporting
- Strongest current source: Danish NVU decisions archive plus the two 2024 annual reports
- Publication owner: NVU and its secretariat at Uddannelses- og Forskningsstyrelsen
- Archive scope: National misconduct decisions in the current NVU regime plus predecessor-regime decisions retained in the archive
- Archive continuity: Current archive preserves predecessor-regime decisions alongside current NVU material
- Referral path: NVU can close the misconduct question and send possible QRP issues back to the relevant institution
- Publication class: Structured archive plus annual-report synthesis
- Accessibility status: Danish archive and annual reports were live in this pass; the English decisions page was live but incomplete
- Case-file status: Strong national misconduct case visibility through anonymized decision PDFs; separate national QRP visibility through annual reporting rather than a decision archive
- Publication basis: Statutory publication practice under the Act and the Board's rules of procedure, plus official annual reports
- Publication exclusions: The English page is not the full current archive; no separate national QRP decision archive identified
- Publication rule or retention: The rules of procedure state that decisions and annual reports are published on the Board's website

- Lane: Questionable research practice

- Publication owner: Each institution for local route pages; NVU for the national annual-report synthesis
- Archive scope: Institution-handled QRP cases reported into the annual national overview
- Archive continuity: Current institutional reporting layer only; no predecessor archive identified
- Referral path: Receives local complaints and returned NVU matters, then reports handled QRP upward through the national annual-report layer
- Publication class: Annual-report summaries
- Accessibility status: National synthesis is live in this pass; local case-file visibility remains uneven
- Case-file status: Denmark has a national public trace of local QRP through annual reporting, but no general national archive of institution-level QRP decisions was identified
- Publication basis: Legal reporting duty and official NVU annual-report practice

### Method notes from the pilot

- Archive continuity needs its own field when a current official archive also holds predecessor-body decisions.
- A single national publication owner can run two different evidence layers at once: direct decisions and institution-reported annual synthesis.
- Referral or return paths between the national board and institutions are part of the case-file story and should be coded, not left implicit.
- A translated official archive page may be a historical or partial mirror rather than the operational archive that should anchor the country coding.

### Gaps for next pass

- Test which universities besides UCPH publish annual reports, minutes or case summaries that go beyond procedure-only pages.
- Check whether Aarhus publishes its annual summary reports publicly or only submits them to NVU.
- Widen the institutional sample across DTU, SDU, AAU, CBS, RUC and non-university research institutions.
- Trace funder consequences more directly from current DFF, Innovation Fund Denmark and DNRF materials into named case-handling routes.
- Keep health ethics, animal research, data protection and URIS separate while checking whether any of them generate public decision practice that affects integrity cases directly.
