# Azerbaijan first expanded dossier sources

Accessed: 28 April 2026

## System reading

Azerbaijan is best read as a degree-attestation and institutional-route system rather than a single national general research-misconduct board.

The strongest national route is the Higher Attestation Commission under the President of the Republic of Azerbaijan (AAK). AAK controls scientific degrees, academic titles, dissertation councils, expert review, appeals, foreign-degree recognition and attestation consequences. It publishes dissertation-defense metadata, dissertation and abstract records, scientific-degree recognition rows, regulations, news and selected case-like notices.

The strongest public case-level trace found in this pass is AAK's 30 September 2025 notice on a plagiarism-based deprivation decision of 8 May 2024, followed by a 19 September 2025 AAK Presidium decision annulling the deprivation after a 10 July 2025 Supreme Court decision. This is a selective attestation case notice, not a comprehensive misconduct archive.

TKTA is a quality-assurance corridor. Its accreditation reports, annual reports and recognition/refusal pages should be coded as QA and qualification-recognition transparency unless a specific file records research-integrity adjudication.

University evidence is mostly procedural: UNEC publishes scientific-plagiarism rules, Azerbaijan University describes an ethics-policy route, ADA exposes QA/accreditation guidance, and university journals publish publication-ethics rules. Public institutional misconduct decisions were not found in this first pass.

Clinical research, physician-publication ethics, data protection, IP, open science and animal research are boundary lanes. They should not upgrade the general research-misconduct transparency score.
