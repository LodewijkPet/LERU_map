# Turkey deep-dive sources

Updated: 24 April 2026

## System reading

Turkey is best read as a centrally regulated committee ecology rather than a single national misconduct board.

- Law No. 2547 and YOK provide the higher-education backbone.
- YOK's directive is the main national rulebook for university research and publication ethics and still leaves most first-line implementation to institutions.
- UAK is a distinct national second-line route because ethics findings can directly affect docentlik applications.
- TUBITAK and TUSEB each run their own AYEK regimes for projects, publications and review or evaluation work.
- TITCK clinical ethics and HADMEK or HADYEK animal ethics are strong parallel boundary regimes and should stay separate from the general misconduct lane.
- Ulusal Tez Merkezi, open-science policy and AI guidance matter as transparency and governance infrastructure even though they are not misconduct tribunals.

## Key conclusions for coding

1. Code Turkey as a centrally regulated but multi-channel system, not as one national misconduct tribunal.
2. Keep the ordinary university route and the UAK docentlik route separate.
3. Treat TUBITAK and TUSEB AYEK as genuine parallel agency integrity systems rather than subparts of the YOK route.
4. Keep TITCK clinical ethics and HADMEK or HADYEK animal ethics visible as separate specialist approval systems.
5. Use institutional front-door pages, directive PDFs, forms, committee lists and registry pages as the main public evidence layer.
6. Keep thesis, open-science, data-protection and AI-guidance material visible as supporting governance infrastructure.

## Public-transparency reading

No national searchable archive of general research-integrity findings was identified in this pass.

Public visibility is strongest for:

- laws, directives and regulations
- UAK workflow and FAQ material
- agency AYEK regulations
- university committee pages and route documents
- TITCK committee-status publication
- HADMEK's local-board registry and application pages
- thesis, open-science and AI-guidance infrastructure

This means Turkey is publicly legible mainly through procedures, committee architecture, forms, membership or status lists and official guidance rather than named case publication.

### Case-publication finding

The main result is a useful contrast: Turkey has a clear RI handling network but no clear public general-misconduct case-reporting archive.

- YOK gives the national template and requires ethics-violation decisions to be reported to YOK within one month, but board decisions are confidential outside general regulations and specified parties.
- UAK exposes a national promotion-linked ethics route and concrete applicant consequences, but no official named or searchable ethics-decision archive was found.
- TUBITAK and TUSEB publish detailed AYEK procedure, appeal and confidentiality rules; the TUBITAK 2026 UBYT procedure adds publication-incentive consequences and AYEK referral logic, but no public AYEK case list was found.
- Necmettin Erbakan University's legal office is a rare official institutional micro-publication source: it publishes ethics-review examples, precedent Council of State decisions and directive files. This is guidance and case-law support, not a live archive of local misconduct findings.
- GIBTU's 28 August 2025 science and engineering board pages provide current evidence of local implementation under the YOK framework, including application-triggered and ex officio review language and monthly meetings when applications exist.
- TITCK activity-status lists and HADMEK/HADYEK registries are strong boundary transparency sources, but they show committee recognition and local-board infrastructure rather than general RI misconduct cases.
