# Bosnia and Herzegovina deep-dive sources

Access date: 2026-04-24

## Committee-and-case-file pass

Bosnia and Herzegovina remains a competence-layered and distributed system, not a single national research-misconduct-board model. The 24 April 2026 pass did not locate a country-wide public misconduct decision archive. The strongest public route evidence is instead a combination of:

- HEA's live accreditation register and institution documentation links, updated 6 April 2026, which expose accreditation status and supporting files rather than misconduct decisions.
- University and faculty ethics routes, especially the University of Sarajevo Ethics Council, member ethics boards and the Faculty of Electrical Engineering public opinions page.
- Public research-approval micro-output at the University of Sarajevo Faculty of Electrical Engineering and the University of Mostar Faculty of Humanities and Social Sciences archive.
- Health, clinical-trial, animal-research, child-research and data-protection routes that are visible but must be kept separate from general research-misconduct case publication.

## Core route findings

### State and quality-assurance route

The Agency for Development of Higher Education and Quality Assurance publishes a state register of accredited higher-education institutions. The list says HEA maintains the State Register, that documentation for a specific institution is available by clicking the institution name, and that the list is regularly updated; the page visible in this pass carried an update date of 6 April 2026. This is a strong accreditation transparency route, but not a misconduct case archive.

HEA's accreditation documents page also exposes the procedural backbone: register rules, appeal-procedure recommendations, expert-commission formation, accreditation criteria, minimum standards and report-writing methodology. These are useful for checking whether ethics, complaints and quality systems are inspected, but they remain quality-assurance evidence.

### University of Sarajevo route

UNSA now has a clearer public ethics route than the earlier dossier reflected. The UNSA Ethics Council page brings together the Code of Ethics, the 29 September 2021 amendment, a 16 July 2025 supplement, procedure/rule documents, recommendations, a first-instance request form and a second-instance appeal form to the Ethics Council.

The 26 February 2025 Senate minutes record acceptance of the Ethics Council's 2023 work report. The report itself was not located as a public standalone file in this pass, so the public evidence is an annual-report trace rather than a full annual-report archive.

The UNSA Faculty of Electrical Engineering publishes a page of Ethics Board opinions, including opinions dated 5 March 2024, 25 April 2024, 24 March 2025, 10 September 2025, 13 November 2025 and 25 February 2026. A sampled 25 February 2026 opinion concerns ethical compatibility of student survey research on artificial-intelligence tool use and records a positive opinion. The page is a real public micro-publication stream, but most files are project/research-ethics opinions and should not be upgraded into general misconduct case decisions.

The UNSA Faculty of Medicine continues to show two different committee routes: a Faculty Ethics Committee for academic ethics/code handling and a Research Ethics Committee for scientific/professional research involving human subjects and laboratory animal models.

### University of Banja Luka route

UNIBL's official "other acts" page publishes the 29 September 2022 Professional Ethics Code and later AI, affiliation, corruption-reporting and administrative documents. The code names an Ethics Committee, says its work is public unless justified reasons exclude the public, gives it authority to decide requests and issue opinions on compatibility with the code, and requires regular annual reports to the Senate. No public Ethics Committee decision or annual-report archive was located in this pass.

### University of Mostar route

The University of Mostar remains a QA-linked institutional route, but the pass found additional micro-publication evidence at faculty level. The archived Faculty of Humanities and Social Sciences psychology pages publicly list ethics-committee approvals for student research topics. The current School of Medicine page exposes the Ethics Committee and required application/consent documents. These are approval-list and procedure traces, not general misconduct findings.

### Clinical, animal, child and data boundaries

The Agency for Medicinal Products and Medical Devices publishes a clinical-trials page, a public clinical-trials register endpoint, an Agency Clinical Trials Commission calendar through 2026, and a patient handbook saying clinical trials pass two stages: health-institution ethics committee opinion followed by Agency assessment. The patient handbook also says all approved studies are public on the Agency website. This is a strong boundary register but not general research-integrity transparency.

The Veterinary Office animal health and welfare department page identifies animal-welfare inspection and legal-control functions. The experimental-animal rulebook requires animal experiments to be reported to the Veterinary Office, restricts which legal persons may conduct experiments, requires project-specific approval, allows special refusal/approval for severe pain cases, and creates a records route. No public experiment-authorisation archive was located.

The 30 May 2025 child-rights platform page records presentation of the revised Ethical Code for research with and about children and frames it as a document protecting the child's personal integrity in research. This remains a thematic vulnerable-participant ethics lane.

## Coding implication

Code Bosnia and Herzegovina as a distributed system with low-to-fragmented public case visibility: no national general misconduct archive, but meaningful official micro-publication through UNSA faculty opinions and Mostar approval lists, annual-report trace evidence through UNSA Senate minutes, accreditation visibility through HEA, and strong boundary lanes for clinical trials, animal research, child research and data protection.
