# Ireland deep-dive sources

Updated: 30 April 2026

## System reading

Ireland is best read as a nationally harmonised but institutionally executed research-integrity system.

- The national layer sits in the National Policy Statement, NRIF guidance, annual statistics and sector-wide training or collaboration infrastructure.
- The actual handling of allegations remains local through Research Integrity Officers, institutional procedures, panels, decision-makers and appeals.
- NRIF should not be coded as a national misconduct board. It is a coordination, guidance, monitoring and exchange layer.
- Ireland's annual national statistics on formal investigations are one of the strongest public transparency features in the dossier.
- Funders matter because Research Ireland and legacy SFI or IRC routes, HRB and EPA all reinforce the host-institution model through grant terms, notification duties and sanctions.
- The health-research ethics and data-consent system is highly visible through NRECs and HRCDC, but it remains analytically separate from misconduct handling.
- Animal-research regulation, academic integrity and open-research policy are boundary or adjacent layers, not the core allegation route.

## Key conclusions for coding

1. Code Ireland as a forum-and-RIO system, not as a centralized national misconduct-board model.
2. Keep NRIF in the coordination, guidance and monitoring layer rather than the adjudication layer.
3. Treat the annual national statistics reports as a real transparency mechanism, even though they do not amount to a decision archive.
4. Keep Research Ireland and legacy SFI or IRC material visible because the funder layer reinforces the host-institution model.
5. Keep NRECs, HRCDC, HPRA and QQI or NAIN clearly separate as boundary or adjacent regimes.
6. The RIO should remain the distinctive Irish local role type across institutions.

## Public-transparency reading

Ireland is not a no-publication system.

Public visibility is strongest for:

- the national policy statement and guidance documents
- annual national statistics on formal investigations
- named RIO or institutional contact routes
- institutional procedure and policy bundles
- funder policy documents
- specialist ethics and regulatory boundary pages

No national searchable archive of individual misconduct decisions was identified in this pass.

The most accurate transparency reading is therefore:

- stronger than a no-publication model because Ireland publishes recurring national statistics and strong local route design
- weaker than a true archive model because reporting remains anonymized, summary-based and distributed

## Quality-consolidation pass - 29 April 2026

### Source currency

- The IUA/NRIF page remains the live national portal and still lists annual statistics reports only for 2016 through 2023. No 2024 national statistics report was located in this pass.
- The 2024 National Policy Statement remains the current national code baseline. It aligns Ireland with the 2023 ALLEA European Code and preserves the institution-led allegation-handling model.
- Research Ireland's current Grant General Terms and Conditions are now the strongest post-merger funder source. They require compliance with the European Code, the National Policy Statement and Research Ireland policy documents; require research-integrity training within six months; require publicised misconduct-identification and investigation mechanisms; and require Research Ireland notification of suspected, alleged and found research misconduct within 30-day reporting windows.
- Research Ireland's Reporting Compliance policy applies to reporting submissions due from 1 December 2025 and adds a separate funder-compliance sanctions lane for late annual, final, progress or research-output reporting. This is compliance visibility, not misconduct case publication.

### ENRIO status

- ENRIO has a country report for Ireland, last updated March 2024.
- ENRIO confirms the key app coding: NRIF is an advisory/support platform and not a misconduct adjudicator; RPOs remain responsible for handling cases; funders expect host institutions to have procedures; and public transparency is mainly through annual consolidated statistics.
- The ENRIO 2024 annual report lists the Health Research Board as Ireland's ENRIO member. Treat this as network evidence, not as evidence of a national misconduct-board function.

### Code baseline

- Main national code: National Policy Statement on Ensuring Research Integrity in Ireland, revised December 2024.

## Committee and case-file pass - 24 April 2026

### Strongest public case-material route

Ireland's closest equivalent to a case dataset is the NRIF annual statistics archive hosted through the IUA research-integrity page, not a list of reasoned case rulings.

- The IUA page states that NRIF publishes annual statistics on the number and types of formal research-misconduct investigations dealt with through formal mechanisms in state-funded research-performing organisations.
- The same page links annual statistics reports for 2016 through 2023.
- The 2023 report says the NRIF agreed in April 2018 to publish annual consolidated reports on formal investigations concluded in the preceding calendar year.
- The 2023 report records five formal investigations concluded in 2023, three upheld, and categories of plagiarism, data-related misconduct, research-practice misconduct and publication-related misconduct.
- The 2023 report identifies the responding RPO universe in an annex: ATU, DCU, DIAS, Marine Institute, MIC, MTU, Maynooth, NIBRT, RCSI, SETU, Teagasc, TUD, TUS, Trinity, UCC, UCD, UL and University of Galway.

Coding implication: this is a durable annual national summary corridor and should score above pure procedure-only visibility, but it is not an ETH Zurich or LOWI-style repository because it does not publish individual decisions, reports, institution names or case reasoning.

### Current funder route after Research Ireland

Research Ireland's Grant General Terms and Conditions now give the clearest post-merger funder route. They define Research Ireland policy documents as potentially including research integrity, data management, animal use, clinical trials and open access, define research misconduct by reference to the National Policy Statement and European Code, and require grant holders to conduct work to the required standard and quality. They also preserve sanction and consent controls around investigations, determinations and communications.

Coding implication: Research Ireland should now be the current funder entry, with SFI and IRC as legacy or transition evidence. The current funder lane is compliance, notification, sanction and communication control rather than public case publication.

### Boundary route check

Health and animal routes remain more visible than general misconduct in some respects, but they should remain separate:

- NREC pages and FAQ explain the national clinical-trial/medical-device/IVD ethics remits, single national ethics opinions and CTIS route for clinical trials. These are ethics-review outputs, not research-misconduct findings.
- HPRA annual reports expose animal-research authorisation and retrospective-assessment statistics. These are regulatory boundary statistics, not general research-integrity case files.
- QQI/NAIN remains the student academic-integrity boundary and should not be merged with research-misconduct handling unless a case is explicitly research-facing.

### Updated public-transparency reading

Ireland should be coded as a summary-based national reporting system with procedure-rich local institutional routes:

- stronger than `Body, no archive` because NRIF publishes a recurring national formal-investigation statistics archive
- weaker than fragmented case-file countries because the public trail lacks individual investigation reports, anonymized decisions, named cases or a searchable institutional repository
- particularly useful for cross-country variables on annual aggregate reporting, RIO roles, institutional procedure design and the difference between research integrity and research ethics approval

### Indexing conclusion

The NRIF annual-statistics series is now indexed at a country-comparison level. Across the published 2016-2023 reports, Ireland exposes recurring national counts, outcome fields and category labels, but no institution names, reasoned decisions, anonymized case narratives or searchable case repository. The indexing strengthens Ireland's summary-based transparency classification and does not justify upgrading it to a decision-archive or fragmented case-file model.

Publication-related misconduct is the clearest repeated category signal: the 2023 report says it is the most frequently observed category across the 2016-2023 series, with 10 category appearances and 6 upheld-category appearances in the report's cumulative charts. Treat those cumulative category figures as chart-level aggregates, not individual case files.
