# Poland deep-dive sources

Updated: 23 April 2026

## System reading

Poland is best read as a legally anchored matrix rather than a single-board national integrity system.

- The 2018 Law on Higher Education and Science is the core frame for the whole system.
- The strongest national ethics route sits at the Committee on Ethics in Science of the Polish Academy of Sciences PAN.
- PAN is not a universal first-instance misconduct court, but its opinions carry special legal weight in disciplinary adjudication and its code is the clearest current national anchor text.
- Practical first-line handling remains local through disciplinary ombudspersons, disciplinary committees, university research-ethics or bioethics committees, and institute support structures.
- NCN is the strongest operational integrity actor in the funding layer because it combines a code, investigation procedure, reporting route, committee structure, ethics guidance and sanctions.
- Poland also has a distinctive digital integrity-support layer through POL-on, the national repository of theses and the Uniform Anti-plagiarism System.
- Clinical trials, animal research and data protection are strong parallel regimes and should remain separated from the general misconduct route.

## Key conclusions for coding

1. Code Poland as a law plus PAN plus local-implementation system, not as one universal national complaints board.
2. Keep PAN visible as the strongest national ethics authority because its opinions have special procedural weight and its code anchors the normative layer.
3. Keep local disciplinary and ethics structures visible because they are where many first-line processes actually happen.
4. Treat NCN as the strongest operational enforcement actor in the funder layer.
5. Keep POL-on, the thesis repository and JSA visible as a distinct infrastructure layer rather than treating them as mere background administration.
6. Keep clinical-trial bioethics, animal research and data protection separate from the general misconduct chain.

## Public-transparency reading

No national searchable archive of research-misconduct findings was identified in this pass.

Public visibility is strongest for:

- law and committee mandates
- PAN codes, regulations, statements and reports
- NCN procedures, codes and orders
- POL-on and thesis-repository infrastructure pages
- university and institute committee pages
- specialist bioethics, animal and data-protection sources

That means Poland is publicly legible mainly through rules, procedures, system infrastructure and committee pages rather than through a searchable archive.

## Committee-and-case-file pass added 23 April 2026

This pass changed the Poland reading in five ways.

1. PAN is not only a code-and-rules source. Its 2023 activity report and 2024 plenary notes show a real national case-trace lane through aggregate counts, categories of allegations and anonymized or generalized case descriptions.
2. Poland should therefore be coded as stronger than a pure rules-only or infrastructure-only system, but still weaker than a searchable national archive of case files.
3. Ministerial disciplinary ombuds, the ministerial disciplinary commission, the research-institute second-instance commission and the RGNiSW disciplinary commission are important route nodes even though no public decision archive was found for them in this pass.
4. The University of Warsaw disciplinary communication is useful as an institutional micro-publication node: it shows that case-specific local notices can appear, but it does not amount to a stable local archive.
5. The Institute of Psychology PAN widens the institutional sample on the PAN side and shows that PAN institutes can have their own visible ethics-committee routes alongside the PAN-wide ethics body.
