# Cyprus committee-and-case-file source notes

Access date: 2026-04-24

## Coding conclusion

Cyprus should not be treated as a LOWI-style or national research-misconduct archive system. The strongest public outputs located in this pass are adjacent lanes:

- CNBC publishes review-committee material, opinions, annual reports and research-proposal decision/register files, including public links for 2020, 2021, 2022 and 2023 research proposals submitted for full bioethical assessment.
- CYQAA publishes accreditation and rejection tables with external evaluation reports, institution comments and final reports, and its complaints policy says formal warning statements and final complaint decisions are published on the relevant entry. The 2024 ENQA review reports aggregate complaint handling for 2018-2022.
- RIF publishes annual reports and routes whistleblowing/complaints to general competent authorities, but no RIF research-misconduct case-publication lane was found.
- Institutional sources at UCY, UNIC and others expose codes, ethics committees, audit/complaint routes and research-governance pages, but no public institutional research-misconduct decision repository was located in this pass.

Transparency coding: score 1, low general research-misconduct publication with bioethics and quality-assurance report lanes. The case-file fragments are important and should be harvested, but they must remain separated from general research-integrity findings.

## Public report and decision lanes located

### Cyprus National Bioethics Committee

- CNBC homepage: https://www.bioethics.gov.cy/
  - Public hub for forms, application guidance, the 17 June 2022 decree on review-committee codes of practice, 2025 meeting schedules, and latest-news links to decision/register files.
  - Latest-news links found for decisions on research proposals submitted for full bioethical assessment in 2020, 2021, 2022 and 2023.
- CNBC 2023 research-proposal decisions page: https://www.bioethics.gov.cy/moh/cnbc/cnbc.nsf/09b538d0121cdc06c2257cbb0034be47/32b46c409d34cf6fc2258bb3002ec79b?OpenDocument
  - Page links to `MITROO EREYNITIKON PROTASEON_2023.pdf`.
  - Code as a project-approval/assessment register, not a misconduct decision.
- CNBC opinions archive: https://www.bioethics.gov.cy/moh/cnbc/cnbc.nsf/dmlopinions_arch_gr/dmlopinions_arch_gr?Count=1000&Expand=3&OpenDocument=&Start=1
  - Official opinion archive with year groupings from 2003 through 2026 in this pass.
  - Normative opinions, not complaint findings.
- CNBC annual reports: https://www.bioethics.gov.cy/moh/cnbc/cnbc.nsf/DMLannualreports_gr/DMLannualreports_gr?OpenDocument=&print=
  - Annual reports from 2002-2006 through 18 July 2019-31 December 2022.
  - Boundary/ethics activity reporting; not a general misconduct archive.
- CNBC operational codes/forms: https://www.bioethics.gov.cy/moh/cnbc/cnbc.nsf/dmlform_en/dmlform_en?OpenDocument=&print=
  - Includes the 2022 code route, decision form EEBK04, monitoring form EEBK05, completion form EEBK06, unexpected interruption EEBK08 and serious-event form EEBK09.

### CYQAA

- CYQAA complaints policy: https://www.dipae.ac.cy/index.php/en/cyqaa-en/complaints-policy-en
  - Complaints can concern accreditation compliance, ESG/WFME standards, evaluation-process integrity or CYQAA process issues.
  - The policy says formal warnings are published on the entry of the institution/department/programme, and final decisions are published for major substantiated complaints.
- CYQAA rejected institutions: https://www.dipae.ac.cy/index.php/en/accreditation-en/rejected-institutions-en
  - Public list with external evaluation reports, institution comments and CYQAA final reports.
- CYQAA rejected programmes: https://www.dipae.ac.cy/index.php/en/accreditation-en/rejected-programmes-en
  - Larger public list of rejected programmes with external evaluation reports, institution comments and final reports.
- CYQAA doctorate programme table: https://www.dipae.ac.cy/index.php/en/accreditation-en/accredited-programmes-doctorate-en
  - Example of public programme-level report tables relevant to research training, including doctoral programmes and report links.
- ENQA agency review 2024: https://www.dipae.ac.cy/archeia/ektheseis/alles_ektheseis/enqacyqaa_external_review_report2024.pdf
  - Reports that all external evaluation reports are published together with CYQAA final reports.
  - Reports complaint-handling aggregate for 2018-2022: 18 complaints filed; 11 substantiated/addressed; six formal recommendations; one referred to police.

### RIF and institutional routes

- RIF annual reports page: https://www.research.org.cy/en/rif/the-foundation/
  - Public annual reports through 2024 found in this pass. Useful funder accountability lane but no misconduct case reporting found.
- RIF complaints/competent authorities page: https://www.research.org.cy/en/investigation-of-complaints-competent-authorities-for-receiving-and-investigating-complaints/
  - Routes whistleblowing and complaints to the Audit Office, Ombudsman and Independent Authority Against Corruption. This is a general governance lane, not a research-misconduct route.
- University of Cyprus internal audit activities: https://www.ucy.ac.cy/internalaudit/activities/?lang=en
  - Includes special investigations, complaint investigation and research-project financial/compliance audits. No public research-misconduct case outputs located.
- University of Nicosia UREC: https://www.unic.ac.cy/research/research-committees/university-research-ethics-committee/
  - Institutional REC route with conflict-of-interest and expert-review provisions. No public opinion or case archive located.

## Searches and exclusions

- Searches on UCY, UNIC, CUT and EUC did not locate a public institutional research-misconduct decision archive.
- A Cyprus Media Complaints Commission ruling and court material referencing a University of Cyprus plagiarism allegation were located, but they are media/legal-route fragments rather than official research-integrity case handling. They were not used for the official source backbone.
- CYQAA and CNBC outputs are useful report/case-like files, but they should be coded as boundary lanes: quality assurance and bioethics approval/opinion, not general misconduct adjudication.
