Updated 23 April 2026 for the committee-and-case-file pilot.

## Committee and case-file pass

### Pilot framing

- The Netherlands was used as the first pilot because the dossier already mapped the committee network and the public case layer is unusually visible.
- The pilot confirms that Dutch visibility is not a one-body story. LOWI, UNL, VH and NWO each expose a different publication layer.
- The key methodological lesson is to separate the handling body from the publication owner.

### Seed bodies

- Body: LOWI
  Type: National second-line advisory body
  Role: Reviews institutional rulings and publishes anonymized advisory opinions
  Strongest current source: `https://lowi.nl/en/opinions/`
  Publication owner: LOWI
  Publication class: Structured archive
  Case-file status: Strong public archive of advisory opinions and inadmissibility decisions
  Publication rule or retention: LOWI Regulations 2022 require anonymized website publication after the final ruling

- Body: UNL
  Type: University sector publication hub
  Role: Publishes anonymized executive-board decisions and CWI advice for research universities
  Strongest current source: `https://www.universiteitenvannederland.nl/publicaties-klachten-wetenschappelijke-integriteit`
  Publication owner: UNL
  Publication class: Structured archive
  Case-file status: Strong yearly archive of university case PDFs
  Publication rule or retention: Sector publication practice in place since 2014

- Body: Vereniging Hogescholen
  Type: Universities-of-applied-sciences sector publication hub
  Role: Publishes final board decisions and linked hbo case files
  Strongest current source: `https://www.vereniginghogescholen.nl/kennisbank/praktijk-en-onderzoek/artikelen/publicatie-klachten-wetenschappelijke-integriteit-2025`
  Publication owner: VH
  Publication class: Stable case page
  Case-file status: Public yearly page with linked PDFs
  Publication rule or retention: Official format sets the fields, six-week publication deadline and ten-year retention

  Type: Funder safety-net route

  Strongest current source: `https://www.nwo.nl/en/scientific-integrity`

  Type: Funder compliance route
  Role: Requires immediate notification and document handover if a funded project's integrity is breached
  Strongest current source: `https://www.zonmw.nl/en/laws-and-regulations-concerning-zonmw`
  Publication owner: Not identified
  Publication class: Procedure only
  Case-file status: No public case archive identified in this pass
  Publication rule or retention: Notification duty is public; publication duty not identified

### Method notes from the pilot

- The publication owner can differ from the institution that handled the complaint. In the Netherlands, institutions and boards handle cases, but UNL or VH may publish them.
- The country should be coded as a publication ladder rather than as `LOWI only`.
- Official format and retention documents are valuable sources because they show whether publication is systematic or ad hoc.
- Even in a transparent country, many institutional pages show procedure and committee composition without maintaining a local case archive.

- ENRIO has a Netherlands country report, last updated April 2024. ### Main code baseline

- The Netherlands Code of Conduct for Research Integrity 2018 remains the active operating code in the official NWO-I material checked in this pass.
- The 2024 evaluation report recommends revision while preserving the code's focus on research integrity and keeping adjacent issues such as generative AI, open science, data protection, social safety and knowledge security from over-expanding the core code.
- NWO-I's 28 September 2025 consultation news confirms that a draft update was opened for user feedback and that the final updated code was expected to enter into force at the beginning of 2026. No final replacement code was located in this 30 April 2026 pass, so the app should keep the 2018 NGWI as the active baseline while flagging the 2026 update watchpoint.
- LOWI's documents page also points to the ALLEA European Code of Conduct for Research Integrity, revised edition 2023, as the most relevant international code for the Netherlands.

### Route and output directory seed

- LOWI: structured national second-line archive; public elements are anonymized advisory opinions and inadmissibility decisions, plus annual reports. Extraction target: 2025 opinions and 2024 annual-report fields by route, allegation type, boundary issue, outcome and whether the LOWI advice was followed.
- UNL: university-sector case archive; public elements are year pages and linked anonymized case files. Extraction target: 2025 and 2026 rows by institution, topic, decision label, final-board status, CWI/LOWI interaction and source PDF availability.
- VH: hbo-sector case page plus publication-format rule; public elements are 2022 and 2025 pages, linked PDFs, six-week publication deadline and ten-year website-retention rule. Extraction target: compare hbo case depth and publication fields with UNL.
- NWO: funder safety-net desk; public elements are procedure page and annual reports. Extraction target: annual desk counts, admissibility reasons, referral to institutions, and any grant-related consequence language.
- ZonMw: funder and LOWI-affiliated institution; public elements are notification and document-handover duties in funded projects. Extraction target: confirm whether any public sanction, default or case-reporting output appears outside general grant compliance.
- NRIN: training and exchange network. Keep as capacity-building evidence, not a complaint route or case-publication owner.

### Boundary targets

- Keep METC/CCMO, animal research, data protection, knowledge security, social safety and IP separate from the NGWI/CWI/LOWI misconduct route.
- LOWI 2025 and 2024 materials repeatedly show boundary pressure points: scientific debate, public communication, METC competence, labour conflict, IP and civil or administrative law. These should be coded as boundary-routing evidence, not as proof that LOWI decides those regimes.
