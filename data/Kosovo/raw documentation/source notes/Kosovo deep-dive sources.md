# Kosovo deep-dive sources

## Committee and case-file pass

Access date: 26 April 2026.

### Seed bodies

- Body: Ministry / MESTI scientific-research framework
- Type: National science-law route
- Lane: Research actor and public-document route
- Role: Identifies public/private research actors, National Science Council context and the legal expectation that research actors publish statutes, rules, decision procedures, quality-control results and ethics codes.
- Strongest current source: Law No. 04/L-135 page and PDF already in the app dossier.
- Publication class: Procedure and public-document duty.
- Case-file status: No misconduct case archive located; use as a harvesting mandate for institutional documents.

- Body: Kosovo Accreditation Agency (KAA), State Council of Quality (SCQ) and General Director
- Type: National quality-assurance route
- Lane: Accreditation, reaccreditation, monitoring, validation, expert reports and SCQ decisions
- Role: KAA is the strongest national public file owner in this pass. It publishes accredited-institution decision pages, expert reports, monitoring reports, post-accreditation reports, annual reports and SCQ meeting traces.
- Strongest current source: KAA decision_v1 page, KAA reports page, 2025 annual work report, monitoring reports page and post-accreditation reports page.
- Publication owner: KAA.
- Archive scope: QA decisions and reports by institution/programme, annual-report summaries and monitoring files.
- Database function: QA decision/report hub, annual-report corridor and monitoring report hub.
- Individual case visibility: Accreditation and implementation files, not general misconduct findings.
- Why this is or is not a misconduct case file: KAA files can expose ethics policies, plagiarism controls, case-record expectations and implementation gaps, but they are accreditation/QA files unless a source explicitly records a misconduct finding.
- Publication class: Public QA decision/report corridor; aggregate appeal/reporting visibility.

- Body: KAA Appeals Committee
- Type: Procedural appeal route
- Lane: HEI appeals against SCQ decisions
- Role: Handles complaints/appeals by higher-education institutions against SCQ decisions. The live page gives current composition and a 30-working-day handling period, extendable by another 30 days. The 2025 KAA annual report records Appeals Committee activity.
- Strongest current source: KAA Appeals Committee page and 2025 annual report.
- Publication owner: KAA.
- Archive scope: Aggregate annual reporting and route page; individual appeal files not separately located.
- Individual case visibility: Aggregate only in this pass.
- Why this is or is not a misconduct case file: Accreditation-procedure appeal, not a staff/student ethics appeal or general misconduct appeal.
- Publication class: Aggregate statistics / route page.

- Body: KAA Code of Ethics / SCQ ethics-compliance route
- Type: QA-process ethics complaint route
- Lane: Conflicts of interest, false declarations, pressure, forgery, disclosure, IP and professional conduct in external QA
- Role: The Code says formal requests can be initiated by KAA actors, HEI representatives, students, academic community members or the public; SCQ receives, investigates and solves ethics-infringement cases; final resolutions are to be made public on the KAA website.
- Strongest current source: KAA Code of Ethics PDF.
- Publication owner: KAA / SCQ.
- Archive scope: Publication rule exists in the Code, but no distinct public ethics-resolution archive was located.
- Individual case visibility: Procedure/publication rule only in this pass.
- Why this is or is not a misconduct case file: It is a real ethics route but scoped to QA-process conduct, not a national scientific-misconduct board.
- Publication class: Procedure only with publication rule; no public trace found for actual resolutions.

- Body: University of Prishtina Ethics Council
- Type: Institutional ethics route
- Lane: Academic staff and related persons; ethics-code violations; severe disciplinary proposals
- Role: Handles ethics procedures, measures, annual public summaries, plagiarism expert commissions and Senate/Steering Council confirmation routes. The 2 May 2025 notice reports an Ethics Council decision dated 18 April 2025 rejecting claims about a 2022 promotion/conflict-of-interest matter. The 1 July 2025 Senate notice reports approval of disciplinary measures against some professors.
- Strongest current source: UP Ethics Council procedure PDF, UP 2 May 2025 ethics-decision notice and UP 1 July 2025 new-code notice.
- Publication owner: UP.
- Archive scope: Isolated public notices plus annual-summary duty; no public decision archive located.
- Individual case visibility: Isolated institutional case notice.
- Why this is or is not a misconduct case file: The 2 May 2025 notice is a real ethics-case trace, but it is not a full decision archive and appears tied to promotion/conflict-of-interest allegations rather than a broad research-misconduct repository.
- Publication class: Individual case notice / procedure with annual-report duty.

- Body: University of Prishtina Committee for Ethics in Scientific Research
- Type: Research-ethics approval route
- Lane: Human and animal subject research by researchers, students and third parties under UP structures
- Role: The 27 March 2023 UP notice says the committee ensures research is in accordance with good research practice and ethical principles and supports doctoral-programme accreditation.
- Strongest current source: UP research ethics regulation announcement.
- Publication owner: UP.
- Archive scope: Procedure and committee route; no approval list, opinions archive or case-output page located.
- Why this is or is not a misconduct case file: Research-ethics approval/protection route, not general misconduct handling.
- Publication class: Procedure only in this pass.

- Body: Other HEIs visible through KAA expert reports
- Type: Institutional implementation evidence
- Lane: Ethics councils, disciplinary commissions, research ethics councils, plagiarism controls and case records
- Role: Ferizaj, Mitrovica and Rezonanca remain useful examples because KAA expert reports show committee designs, awareness gaps and anti-plagiarism implementation issues.
- Strongest current source: KAA institution pages and expert reports already cited in the app dossier.
- Publication owner: KAA for reports; institutions for local procedures.
- Archive scope: QA expert reports by institution/programme.
- Individual case visibility: Usually implementation evidence rather than public misconduct outcomes.
- Publication class: QA report implementation evidence.

- Body: Information and Privacy Agency (AIP)
- Type: Boundary authority
- Lane: Personal data protection and access to public documents
- Role: Supervises Law No. 06/L-082 on personal data and Law No. 06/L-081 on access to public documents; publishes at least a decisions page and action/publication pages.
- Strongest current source: AIP about page, personal data protection page and decisions page.
- Why this is or is not a misconduct case file: Privacy/access route that can affect research records, but not a research-integrity adjudicator.
- Publication class: Boundary decision/publication page.
