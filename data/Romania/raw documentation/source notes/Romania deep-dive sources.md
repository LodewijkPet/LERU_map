# Romania deep-dive sources

Updated: 23 April 2026

## System reading

Romania is best read as a juridified split integrity model rather than as one single national misconduct board.

- The CDI lane runs through Law 183/2024, the renewed 2025 national CDI code architecture, local research-organization ethics commissions, analysis commissions and CNECSDTI as a real national second-line and direct-review body.
- The higher-education lane runs separately through Law 199/2023, the 2024 framework code, the 2024 framework regulation, independent university ethics commissions, research-ethics subcommissions and topic-specific appeals to CNATDCU, CNEMU or CNECSDTI.
- ANC and UEFISCDI matter because they expose the research-governance and consultative-council infrastructure around the integrity system, but they should not be coded as universal case-handling bodies.
- University of Bucharest and Babes-Bolyai University are the strongest public institutional transparency routes in this pass because they expose reports, procedures and public case material.
- POLITEHNICA Bucharest and UMF Carol Davila show how the general university framework is translated in technical and medical settings.
- ICI Bucharest is the clearest non-university CDI example collected in this pass and helps keep Romania from being coded only through university sources.
- ANMDMR and CNBMDM, the personal-data authority, and the animal-use law remain specialist boundary regimes and should not be merged into the core misconduct chain.

## Key conclusions for coding

1. Code Romania as a split CDI and higher-education integrity system, not as a unitary national board.
2. Treat CNECSDTI as a real national second-line and direct-review body in the CDI lane, but not as the handler of all university ethics disputes.
3. Keep Law 199/2023 and the 2024 framework instruments visible as a separate university ethics architecture with mandatory independent commissions and research-ethics subcommissions.
4. Keep CNATDCU and CNEMU in the national appeal and oversight layer rather than in the first-line institutional layer.
5. Use at least one non-university CDI example in the dossier; otherwise Romania looks too university-centric.
6. Keep clinical bioethics, animal-use review and personal-data control as boundary regimes.

## Public-transparency reading

Romania is not a no-publication system.

Public visibility is strongest for:

- law-based publication duties
- national regulations and member-selection notices
- university annual reports
- university solved-case pages
- public decision PDFs at some institutions
- CEMU decisions and procedures
- specialist regulator pages

No single national searchable archive covering both the CDI and higher-education lanes was identified in this pass.

The best reading is therefore:

- stronger than a fragmented no-publication model because publication duties are written into law and some institutions publish substantial case material
- still distributed because the evidence is spread across law portals, ANC notices, university pages, PDF reports and committee subpages
