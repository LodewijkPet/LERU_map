### Pilot finding

- Slovakia should not be coded as "no public decision material". The national layer is aggregate and developmental, but at least two institutions already publish local minutes or opinions.
- NKVIE is strongest as a national aggregate monitoring and review node, not yet as a national archive.
- UPJS and STU are best treated as institutional micro-publication nodes.
- APVV remains a visible procedure route, but no public APVV outcome archive was identified.

### Workflow refinements from Slovakia

- When a national body is new, inspect annual reports and meeting notes for case-volume evidence and for statements about future publication practice.
- Record aggregate national monitoring separately from decision publication; counts and themes are not the same as published case files.
- If no national archive exists, always search institutional pages for `minutes`, `opinions`, `stanoviska`, `zápisnice` or similar committee-output labels.
- Code public minutes and opinions as micro-publication nodes even when they are not fully research-misconduct files, but label their scope carefully.
- Distinguish student academic-integrity, employment, quality-governance and publication-ethics material inside institutional committee outputs rather than collapsing them into one undifferentiated misconduct bucket.
