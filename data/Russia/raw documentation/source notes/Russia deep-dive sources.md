# Russia deep-dive sources

Updated: 24 April 2026

## System reading

Russia is best read as an attestation-centered distributed system rather than as a single national misconduct board.

- The strongest official route sits in the state scientific-attestation system around Federal Law No. 127-FZ, Minobrnauki, the ministry department for state scientific attestation, VAK, dissertation councils and degree consequences.
- Russia also has a separate ministry-monitored lane for organizations allowed to award degrees independently, so the dossier should not collapse everything into one VAK-only route.
- The academy and professional layer matters: the RAS Council on the Ethics of Scientific Research, the RAS anti-falsification commission and ANRI's publication-ethics structures are important supra-institutional actors.
- Universities and funders expose a different layer through conduct pages, IRBs, bioethics committees and RSF declarations or ethics statements.
- Clinical research, animal-use ethics, personal data and IP are important boundary regimes and should stay separate from the main attestation lane.
- Non-state watchdogs such as Dissernet remain relevant for public pressure and discovery, but they are not official adjudicators and were not used as the backbone for this official-source pass.

## Source-access note

Several important Russian official pages were discoverable and indexed but were intermittently inaccessible from the current environment during this pass, especially parts of the live VAK and ministry web stack.

That means the dossier is intentionally conservative:

- where a page opened directly, it was used normally
- where an official page was clearly indexed but unstable, the dossier records the official route and marks the access limitation
- the system is coded from the verified structure rather than from assumed full transparency

This is especially important for VAK portal tabs, dissertation-council visibility and degree-deprivation or order indexing.

## Key conclusions for coding

1. Code Russia as an attestation-centered distributed system, not as a single national misconduct office.
2. Keep VAK, dissertation councils and the autonomous degree-awarding route at the center of the official map.
3. Treat the RAS ethics council as an emerging 2025-2026 layer rather than as a long-settled national code regime.
4. Keep ANRI visible as a professional publication-ethics route, but separate it from state authority.
5. Use institutional pages such as HSE and MSU to show the local route without pretending institutional visibility is uniform.
6. Keep clinical, animal, data and IP regimes clearly separated from the general attestation and publication-ethics chain.

## Public-transparency reading

No unified national searchable archive of general research-integrity findings was identified in this pass.

Public visibility is strongest for:

- law and ministry competence pages
- VAK portal architecture, dissertation-council and reviewed-journal infrastructure
- RAS committee pages and code-development news
- ANRI ethics and publication-ethics materials
- institutional route pages and reports
- clinical-regulatory and funder boundary material

That means Russia is publicly legible mainly through route architecture, registry-style visibility, committee pages and specialist notices rather than through a stable archive of named case decisions.

## Remaining gaps for a final sweep

- A cleaner live VAK source set, especially for dissertation councils, document tabs and degree-deprivation indexing
- Broader institutional coverage beyond the current HSE and MSU sample
- More autonomous-degree organization examples, including clearer university-level route pages
- More systematic extraction of VAK/GIS Nauka orders by decision type and year
- A wider direct national animal-authority trail beyond RSF, MSU and the Rosselkhoznadzor preclinical-study checklist
- Better evidence on whether official case outputs outside the attestation lane are published more systematically than the current pass could verify
