# Hungary deep-dive sources

Updated: 24 April 2026

## System reading

Hungary is best read as a multi-pillar system.

- The clearest general scientific-integrity norm source is the revised 2024 MTA Science Ethics Code.
- That code does not centralize first-line handling. It says the responsibility to conduct the procedure normally lies with the higher-education institution, research institution or employer concerned.
- TeB is therefore a special national route and sometimes a second-line or appeal route, not a universal first-line misconduct board.
- HUN-REN adds a second national public-research-network layer with its own law, conduct-code work, whistleblowing and organisational-integrity machinery.
- NKFIH shapes the policy, evaluation and open-science environment rather than operating as a general misconduct tribunal.
- ODT and MAB form a doctoral and accreditation governance layer that matters for the quality environment around research.
- The most formal national and regional committee system in Hungary sits in biomedical and animal-research ethics, especially through ETT, TUKEB, KFEB, REKEBET, Semmelweis RKEB, OGYEI and NEBIH.

## Committee and case-file pass, 24 April 2026

### Strongest public case-material and archive targets

- **MTA / TeB route.** The MTA Science Ethics Committee page proves the current committee, contact route and competence. The 2024 Science Ethics Code adds a real publication basis: in severe misconduct cases TeB may order publication of a definitive decision in the Official Gazette of the Academy, and it may publish general principle statements on the MTA website without case-identifying detail. No clean public TeB decision archive was found in this pass.
- **MTA aggregate-report corridor.** The 2018 General Assembly news item reports TeB's 2017 activity: three meetings, six submissions examined, five closed and no second-instance Presidium proceeding then pending. The 2024 General Assembly decisions page records acceptance of TeB's 2023 activity report. These are useful aggregate or report-trail sources, not decision archives.
- **TUKEB permit archive.** The TUKEB page is the strongest official extraction target found. It links annual pages for permits issued at meetings from 2010 through 2025, and the 2025 page exposes project titles, applicants and investigators. This is case-like publication for biomedical research-ethics approval, not for general misconduct.
- **Corvinus REC approval decisions.** Corvinus publishes a Research Ethics Committee resolutions page. A representative 2023 decision PDF includes file number, researcher, unit, project goal, approval result, justification and meeting date. This is an institutional approval archive, not a misconduct findings archive.
- **ETT statements archive.** ETT's statements and information page publishes medical-ethics and biomedical-ethics statements, including a 2025 statement on publicity of medical ethics proceedings. This is a boundary opinion/procedure archive.
- **NNGYK / CTIS clinical-trial route.** The current Hungarian medicines-authority page says the national clinical-trial list is no longer updated and points users to the CTIS public interface for trials authorised under Regulation 536/2014/EU. Store it as a boundary register route, not a research-misconduct source.
- **HUN-REN institute implementation.** The central HUN-REN layer remains important, and HUN-REN Centre for Ecological Research's 25 March 2026 organisational regulations provide an additional institute-level implementation source beyond CSFK. No HUN-REN public case archive was found.

### Coding conclusion from this pass

Hungary remains a low-publication general misconduct system with valuable lane-specific archives. The general integrity route is institution-first with MTA/TeB as national norm and special second-line route. TeB has a publication rule and aggregate public reporting, but no directly reachable general decision archive was identified. The extractable archives found in this pass are mostly biomedical permits and institutional research-ethics approvals, so they should be stored for later extraction while remaining separate from misconduct transparency.

## Key conclusions for coding

1. Code Hungary as institution-first for general scientific integrity, with MTA and TeB as the strongest national norm and special second-line layer.
2. Treat HUN-REN as a real governance and compliance layer, not just a neutral network label.
3. Treat NKFIH as a policy, evaluation and open-science actor rather than a general case-handling body.
4. Keep ODT and MAB visible as doctoral and accreditation governance rather than as misconduct tribunals.
5. Show the biomedical and animal systems separately because they are the most formalized national and regional committee routes in the country.

## Public-transparency reading

No general national archive of research-misconduct findings was identified in this pass.

Public visibility is strongest for:

- codes and committee procedures
- committee membership pages
- MTA/TeB publication rules and aggregate report traces
- TUKEB-issued permit lists from 2010 through 2025
- institutional research-ethics approval decisions, especially Corvinus REC resolutions
- ETT biomedical or medical-ethics statements
- CTIS-linked clinical-trial register visibility through NNGYK
- animal-experiment statistics and reporting pages
- institutional committee pages

That means Hungary is more publicly legible through specialist ethics publication than through public misconduct findings.

## Remaining gaps for a final sweep

- Verify whether any TeB definitive decisions are directly reachable through the Academy Gazette or another MTA publication route.
- Index the TUKEB annual permit pages by year and field set.
- Broaden the university sample beyond Corvinus, ELTE and Semmelweis, especially to test whether more institutions publish research-ethics decisions or only procedures.
- Expand HUN-REN institute coverage beyond CSFK and the Centre for Ecological Research and classify whether institute committees publish outputs.
- Keep NNGYK/CTIS, ETT, NEBIH and NAIH boundary records separate from general misconduct publication.
