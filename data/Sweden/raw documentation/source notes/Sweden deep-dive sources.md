# Sweden deep-dive sources

Updated: 23 April 2026

## System reading

Sweden is best read as a public-law two-track integrity system with separate national human-research and animal-research ethics regimes.

- Npof is the national decision body for statutory FFP misconduct.
- Higher education institutions and other research principals remain responsible for the broader category of other deviations from good research practice.
- The Higher Education Ordinance makes local support, guideline and annual-reporting duties unusually explicit.
- EPM and ONEP form a separate national human-research ethics and supervision system that must stay distinct from misconduct handling.
- Swedish funders and guidance actors matter because they translate the legal framework into grant, training and compliance expectations.
- Sweden is one of the stronger public-transparency models because Npof publishes a real decision archive, even though local non-FFP visibility is much weaker.

## Key conclusions for coding

1. Sweden should be coded as a statutory split model, not as a purely centralized misconduct-board system.
2. The national route is narrow and strong: Npof handles FFP misconduct only.
3. The institutional route is broad and essential: other deviations from good research practice stay with the research principal.
4. Human-research ethics and animal ethics are highly visible parallel systems and should not be collapsed into misconduct handling.
5. Sweden's funder and guidance layer is important because law, soft-law guidance and grant terms reinforce each other.
6. Sweden has one of the stronger public-transparency models in the project because of the Npof decision archive, but that strength mainly concerns the national FFP lane.

## Public-transparency reading

Sweden is not just a summary-based system.

Public visibility is strongest for:

- the Npof decision archive
- Npof practice and annual-report pages
- the legal allocation of national and local duties
- institutional procedure pages for other deviations
- separate human-research and animal-research authority pages

The most accurate transparency reading is therefore:

- stronger than a summary-only model because Sweden has a real structured national decision archive
- weaker than a full-system archive model because the broader local category of other deviations from good research practice remains much less publicly visible
