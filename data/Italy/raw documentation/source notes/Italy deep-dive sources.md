# Italy deep-dive sources

Updated: 22 April 2026

## System reading

Italy is best read as a distributed and autonomy-based research-integrity system.

- There is no single national misconduct board for all sectors.
- The legal baseline comes from constitutional freedom of science, university autonomy, Law 240/2010 and the doctoral-accreditation layer around D.M. 226/2021.
- MUR and ANVUR shape the national steering, accreditation and quality environment, but they do not operate as one general case-handling tribunal.
- CNR is the clearest para-national integrity engine. Its Research Ethics and Integrity Committee, documents page, misconduct route and Rome Statement create the strongest public general-integrity backbone above the single-institution level.
- Universities remain the practical first-line layer through their own research-integrity committees, research-ethics committees, charters, regulations, reporting addresses and doctoral rules.
- AIRC and Telethon add a visible private-funder compliance layer through integrity, conflict-of-interest and host-institution expectations.
- The most formal national committee system sits in specialist boundary regimes such as clinical-trial ethics, animal-welfare review, national bioethics and data protection.

## Key conclusions for coding

1. Code Italy as a distributed university-based system, not as a single national misconduct-board model.
2. Treat MUR's 2025 security-and-integrity initiative as a new coordination layer that still depends on institutional implementation.
3. Keep ANVUR visible as a quality-and-accreditation actor rather than a case-handling tribunal.
4. Treat CNR as the strongest para-national general-integrity engine because it combines guidance, training, ethics support and a visible misconduct-reporting route.
5. Keep clinical-trial ethics, animal welfare, data protection and national bioethics separate from the general misconduct chain even though they are often more formalized and publicly legible.

## Public-transparency reading

That means Italy is publicly visible mainly through norms, committees and specialist oversight rather than through published case outcomes.

## Committee and case-file pass

Updated: 23 April 2026

### Pilot finding

Italy is a useful test case for countries with many smaller official publication nodes rather than one national case-file archive.

The pilot confirms the core reading from the deep dossier: no general national research-misconduct case archive was identified. However, Italy has many official micro-publication nodes that can look like case-file evidence if they are not coded carefully. Most of them are boundary-regime or institutional sources rather than general misconduct archives.

### Seed bodies and publication nodes

#### CNR Research Ethics and Integrity Committee / Research Integrity and Misconduct

- Type: para-national general-integrity route
- Lane: CNR and CNR-adjacent research integrity
- Strongest current sources: CNR documents page and CNR Research Integrity and Misconduct page
- Publication owner: CNR
- Archive scope: guidance, opinions, responses, ethical-clearance infrastructure and public description of the allegation route
- Publication depth: public guidance and response/opinion references, but not a searchable archive of final misconduct outcomes
- Publicity mode: proactive web publication for documents; outcome trail remains limited
- Route scope versus archive scope: strongest Italian general-integrity route found, but not a universal national tribunal

The CNR documents page is important because it explicitly includes responses on alleged misconduct cases and advice to CNR departments or institutes. This makes it stronger than a simple code page. It still does not equal a full decision database.

#### AIFA, OsSC and clinical-trial open data

- Type: specialist regulatory and ethics route
- Lane: medicinal-product clinical trials
- Strongest current sources: AIFA clinical-trials open-data page, OsSC page and Clinical Trials Office page
- Publication owner: AIFA / CTIS / OsSC
- Archive scope: clinical-trial metadata, positive and negative evaluation categories, ongoing/concluded/non-approved or withdrawn trial data and operational routing through AIFA and ethics committees
- Publication depth: structured CSV/open-data and register-style visibility, not research-misconduct case reasoning
- Publicity mode: proactive online open-data publication
- Route scope versus archive scope: strong boundary-regime data source, not a general misconduct archive

This is the clearest example from the Italy pass of a real database that must not be mistaken for a research-integrity case-file archive.

#### Ministry of Health animal-research database and non-technical-summary pages

- Type: specialist animal-research authorization route
- Lane: animals used for scientific purposes
- Strongest current sources: Ministry of Health OPBA page, Legislative Decree 26/2014, and official pages for the Banca Dati Telematica della Sperimentazione and Sintesi non tecnica
- Publication owner: Ministry of Health
- Archive scope: authorization and animal-research project/public-summary infrastructure
- Accessibility status: official pages were identified, but command-line access returned browser-validation or cookie-protection pages in this pass
- Route scope versus archive scope: probable project-authorization/public-summary route, but treat the database pages as accessibility-limited until a browser pass or direct download confirms the public fields

This is a useful accessibility lesson: a source can be official and important while still needing a caution note because the page is protected from automated access.

#### UNIVPM Research Ethics Committee annual report

- Type: institutional committee report
- Lane: university research-ethics review
- Strongest current source: 2024 annual report PDF
- Publication owner: UNIVPM
- Archive scope: annual activity reporting for a local research-ethics committee
- Publication depth: annual-report visibility with counts and committee activity, not a misconduct decision archive
- Publicity mode: proactive PDF publication
- Route scope versus archive scope: a local committee transparency node that helps map practice in one institution

UNIVPM is now the best Italian example of an institutional micro-publication node that goes beyond a committee page.

#### University of Milan Ethics Committee

- Type: institutional ethics and integrity-opinion route
- Lane: project ethics review and non-disciplinary ethics/integrity code violations
- Strongest current source: University of Milan Ethics Committee page
- Publication owner: University of Milan
- Archive scope: committee page, meeting calendar/components and restricted Unimibox minutes
- Publication depth: procedure, membership and access-status evidence; minutes are restricted
- Publicity mode: public committee page plus restricted record set
- Route scope versus archive scope: strong negative/publication-exclusion evidence because the university states where minutes exist and who can access them

This source is valuable because it explicitly says the committee can advise on violations of the Code of Ethics and Research Integrity that are not also disciplinary offences, while meeting minutes are restricted.

#### Italian Committee for Bioethics

- Type: national advisory opinion archive
- Lane: bioethics boundary regime
- Strongest current source: opinions and responses archive
- Publication owner: Presidency of the Council of Ministers / Italian Committee for Bioethics
- Archive scope: national bioethics opinions and responses
- Publication depth: public opinions, not project misconduct case files
- Route scope versus archive scope: strong advisory publication archive, but not a misconduct route

#### Garante Privacy decision database

- Type: data-protection decision route
- Lane: personal data and scientific or health research
- Strongest current source: Doc-Web 10057629, decision of 17 July 2024
- Publication owner: Garante per la protezione dei dati personali
- Archive scope: public DPA decisions, including research and health-data cases
- Publication depth: reasoned public data-protection decisions
- Route scope versus archive scope: strong case-level boundary-regime publication, not general research-misconduct adjudication

### Method refinement from Italy

The Italy pass adds a new coding habit for large, distributed systems:

- record the database function, not only whether a database exists
- distinguish misconduct decision archives from approval databases, project registers, opinion archives, annual reports, restricted meeting records and accessibility-limited pages
- treat micro-publication nodes as useful evidence for route mapping, not automatically as proof of case-file transparency
- code restricted minutes or protected pages as evidence, because they explain why the public trail stops
- keep boundary-regime databases visible, but keep them out of the general research-misconduct score unless the source itself connects them to misconduct handling

### Individual case visibility examples

The follow-up check confirms that Italy should not be coded only as "no archive found". The more useful description is: no general national misconduct archive was identified, but several official sources expose scattered case-like or study-level information in different lanes.

#### Sapienza Senate minutes, 10 December 2024

- Source type: institutional governance minutes
- Lane: university ethics and conduct, not a research-misconduct archive
- Public fields visible: date of the Senate meeting, agenda item, deliberation number, committee route, anonymized initials for the teacher involved, broad allegation category, code provisions cited, Senate vote and final formal-warning outcome
- Fields not public or restricted: the underlying Ethics Commission report is explicitly not uploaded to the collegiate-bodies platform because of privacy protection; supporting evidence and full file are not public
- Case-file status: incidental individual case trace in governance minutes
- Why this matters: the source proves that individual ethics/conduct outcomes can surface in university governance records, but it does not create a searchable archive of institutional misconduct files

#### Parma Ethics Committee APIDULCIS study page

- Source type: local clinical-study ethics committee page
- Lane: clinical/biomedical ethics boundary regime
- Public fields visible: study acronym/title, study type, protocol reference, sponsor/promoter, principal investigator or unit, requested opinion/committee action and document status
- Fields not public or restricted: full committee deliberation, reviewer reasoning, study file and any monitoring or non-compliance follow-up are not exposed on the page
- Case-file status: study-level approval/ethics record, not a misconduct case file
- Why this matters: it shows the kind of granular project record that can exist in boundary regimes even when the general research-integrity route publishes no decision files

#### University of Milan Ethics Committee

- Source type: institutional committee page and restricted record notice
- Lane: project ethics and non-disciplinary ethics or research-integrity opinions
- Public fields visible: committee mandate, meeting calendar, current members, procedure and explicit note that minutes exist
- Fields not public or restricted: minutes are available only through restricted Unimibox access
- Case-file status: positive evidence that a record set exists, negative evidence for public access

#### Garante Privacy Doc-Web decision

- Source type: data-protection authority decision
- Lane: personal-data and health/scientific-research boundary regime
- Public fields visible: reasoned decision, legal basis, parties/roles as publishable, facts, authority reasoning, order and sanction/consequence information
- Fields not public or restricted: personal data and confidential details redacted under the authority's publication practice
- Case-file status: real case-level publication in the data-protection lane, not a general research-misconduct file

Coding rule from these examples:

- For every case-like source, record what is public, what is restricted or absent, and why the source is or is not a research-misconduct case file.
- Incidental governance-minutes traces, study-level approval pages, restricted minute notices and regulator decisions should be preserved as evidence, but coded by lane and database function.
