# Belgium deep-dive sources

Accessed: 24 April 2026

## Committee and case-file pass

### Reading note

Belgium should be coded lane by lane. The general country container has a Belgium-wide research-ethics code and federal boundary/compliance actors, but the public research-integrity case trail is mainly community and institutional. The strongest public case material identified in this pass is Flemish: VCWI annual reports at second-advice level, and KU Leuven plus UAntwerpen annual reports at first-line CWI level. The French-speaking/FWB lane is structurally visible through CSIS, FNRS and PINDARE, but no comparable public CSIS case-output archive was located.

### Seed bodies and route status

- VCWI: Flemish second-advice body. It handles complaint files only after local CWI handling is complete. The public publication owner is VCWI through annual reports, not a Belgian national archive.
- VCWI-recognized institutions: the connected-institutions page is the best directory for Flemish local CWI routes. As of this pass it records 22 connected institutions, including Waterbouwkundig Laboratorium from 10 October 2025.
- FWO: funder and VCWI-building route. FWO does not operate its own CWI; allegations are handled by the host institution.
- KU Leuven CWI: strong institutional annual-report lane with counts, allegations, outcomes and anonymized advice summaries.
- UAntwerpen CWI: strong institutional annual-report lane with multi-year table, FFP/OUP classification, formal follow-up, VCWI-advice status and anonymized summaries.
- UGent, UHasselt, VUB and Flemish institutes: route and procedure pages remain extraction targets, but this pass did not identify an equivalent recurring public case-summary series for each.
- CSIS: French-speaking/FWB second-advice structure. FNRS hosts a public information page and regulation, but the dedicated CSIS site was inaccessible in this pass and no public decision/annual case-output archive was found.
- PINDARE/CRef: FWB handler-directory and resource route. Useful for identifying ULiege, UCLouvain, ULB, UMONS, UNamur and CSIS links; not a case-output archive.

### Procedure and directory sources

- VCWI second advice: https://www.vcwi.be/nl/tweede-advies
  - Shows the route is second advice only, after a local CWI procedure, within a 30-day submission window; disciplinary measures remain outside VCWI.

- VCWI connected institutions: https://www.vcwi.be/nl/aangesloten-instellingen
  - Lists Flemish universities, funder and institute routes and is the main expansion directory for later institutional harvesting.

- FWO research integrity: https://www.fwo.be/en/about-fwo/research-policy/research-integrity/
  - Confirms FWO's integrity duties, VCWI relationship and host-institution handling model.

- FNRS CSIS page: https://www.frs-fnrs.be/en/high-council-scientific-integrity-csis
  - Confirms CSIS as the FWB scientific-integrity structure and lists membership/role information.

## Quality-consolidation pass

### Source-currency note

- The live ENRIO country-report archive still contains a Belgium/Flanders report rather than a Belgium-wide national-system report. The report confirms the current app reading: the VCWI is a Flemish second-advice structure, not a Belgian national first-instance board.
- ENRIO's Belgium member page now gives two Belgian institutional entries: VCWI and CSIS. Treat this as membership and comparison evidence, not as proof that Belgium has one combined case-handling route.
- The ENRIO Annual Report 2024 also lists both CSIS and VCWI as Belgian ENRIO members. This supports the two-community coding and corrects any reading that only the Flemish lane is internationally visible.
- The VCWI annual-report hub remains the strongest cross-institutional public-output corridor. It lists annual reports from 2013 through 2025.
- KU Leuven remains a strong first-line annual-report source. Its annual-report hub states that KU Leuven publishes the number and nature of notifications and anonymized summaries; the latest page update located in this pass is 4 July 2025.
- KU Leuven's CRI regulation remains current enough for procedure coding: the English page says the official Dutch regulation was approved by the Executive Board on 24 September 2024 and the page was last updated on 28 October 2025.
- UAntwerpen remains the strongest compact institutional table source located in Belgium. Its CWI page links annual reports from 2018-2019 through 2025.
- The CSIS/FWB lane remains route-visible but output-light. The FNRS CSIS page confirms the CSIS role and composition; the 14 September 2023 CSIS regulation confirms second-advice competence, institutional obligations, confidentiality and the 30-day submission rule. No public CSIS annual case report, decision archive or anonymized opinion list was found in this pass.
- PINDARE remains the best FWB university handler-directory source: it maps ULiege, UCLouvain, ULB, UMONS and UNamur committee routes plus CSIS as the post-CIS second-advice route.

### ENRIO status

- Country report: yes, but scoped to Belgium/Flanders and VCWI. It should be cited as an ENRIO comparison source for the Flemish lane, not as a Belgium-wide national report.
- ENRIO membership: yes. VCWI and CSIS are both listed as Belgian ENRIO member institutions in the ENRIO members page and in the ENRIO Annual Report 2024.
- Effect on app coding: confirms the federal-container / community-system reading. No change to the no-single-national-archive conclusion.

### Main code baseline

- Belgium-wide baseline: Code of Ethics for Scientific Research in Belgium, produced by the four Belgian academies with BELSPO support. It remains a shared national ethical baseline and not a complaint-board source.
- European baseline: ALLEA European Code of Conduct for Research Integrity, revised edition 2023. VCWI and KU Leuven cite the European code; CSIS regulation still names the most recent European code as its primary integrity definition while also referring to the Belgian 2009 code and FNRS 2007 code.
- Funder baseline: FWO's scientific-integrity clause attaches the Belgian ethical code and the European code to calls, application forms and research contracts; FWO research ethics makes ethics approval binding through the grant agreement and auditable up to five years after completion.
- Federal programme baseline: BELSPO P4Science 2024-2025 keeps the 2009 Belgian code active for federal projects and requires an ethics form and, where necessary, institutional ethics-board consultation.

### Route and funder matrix

- VCWI: Flemish second advice after completed local CWI handling; public output through annual reports rather than full decisions.
- VCWI-recognized institutions: main Flemish route directory; use it to widen institutional annual-report and procedure checks.
- FWO: no own CWI; host institutions investigate complaints. FWO contributes through integrity clauses, grant conditions, regulation amendments, ethics checklist duties and VCWI infrastructure.
- CSIS: FWB second advice after institutional CIS handling or a decision not to pronounce; no first-advice competence and no located public case-output archive.
- FNRS: funder and CSIS-building actor; scientific-policy page links integrity to CSIS, open science, DMPs, ethics/transparency and animal experimentation.
- PINDARE/CRef: FWB resource and directory layer; useful for identifying local committees but not a case-output archive.
- KU Leuven: first-line CRI route with reporting desk, advisers, current regulation and public annual reports.
- UAntwerpen: first-line CWI route with contact point, procedure, composition and annual reports through 2025.
- Boundary lanes: BELSPO research security, FAMHP/ethics committees, FWO research ethics, Belgian Data Protection Authority and regional animal-experiment routes must remain separate from general research-misconduct publication.
