# France deep-dive sources

Updated: 29 April 2026

## System reading

France is best read as a legally codified, institutionally executed and nationally coordinated research-integrity network.

- The legal core sits in the Code de la recherche, especially Articles L.211-2, D.211-2, D.211-3 and the Hceres mission article L.114-3-1.
- Institutions remain the practical first-line handlers through the referent a l'integrite scientifique, local procedures, expert input and reporting to the head of institution.
- Hceres and Ofis are the national center of gravity for coordination, observatory work, resources, RIS visibility and harmonisation, but not a routine first-instance misconduct board.
- The national RIS directory is one of the most important public infrastructure sources because it shows the breadth of the French network across universities, operators and the funder layer.
- Inadis should still be read cautiously as a light consultative analysis layer linked to the Academie des sciences, not as a Danish-style national misconduct tribunal.
- Large operators such as CNRS, Inserm and INRAE are crucial because they often publish richer procedure and governance material than universities.
- ANR is a meaningful funder node with its own integrity and deontology framework and should be kept separate from institutional case handling.
- Human research, bioethics, animal experimentation, data protection and deontology remain boundary regimes even though French institutions often present them next to scientific integrity.

## Key conclusions for coding

1. France should be coded as a networked RIS model anchored in law, not as a single national misconduct-board system.
2. Hceres and Ofis are the national center of gravity for coordination and monitoring, but routine case handling remains institutional.
3. The RIS directory is a core source type in its own right because France exposes the handler network publicly.
4. Ofis synthesis work means France is not a no-publication system, even though it still lacks a searchable archive of individual decisions.
5. Inadis should remain an emerging consultative review layer until a clearer stand-alone official portal or procedure appears.
6. Operator and funder routes need to stay visible because they add important public procedure detail beyond generic university pages.

## Public-transparency reading

France is best read as a summary-based publication model rather than as either a full archive system or a pure no-publication system.

Public visibility is strongest for:

- laws and decrees
- named RIS routes
- Ofis guidance and monitoring pages
- the national 2022-2023 synthesis of handled integrity matters
- institutional annual activity and procedure pages
- operator and funder integrity pages

No searchable national archive of individual misconduct decisions was identified in this pass.

The most accurate coding is therefore:

- stronger than a no-publication model because Ofis publishes national summary evidence and some institutions publish annual activity material
- weaker than a true archive model because the public layer remains mostly aggregate, procedural and distributed

## Committee and case-file pass added 23 April 2026

### Public route reading

France has a layered public record rather than one case-file archive.

- Ofis national monitoring: the 2022-2023 synthesis is the strongest national public source, but it is aggregate reporting from institutions, not file-by-file publication.
- Ofis RIS directory: the annuaire recorded 187 named RIS on 23 April 2026 and is the live handler map; it should not be coded as case-output publication.
- Inadis: the Ofis July 2025 recommendations and Academie des sciences working-group page support coding Inadis as a national second-look or file-analysis route. It is not yet evidence of a public decision archive.
- CNRS/MIS reports: the MIS reports page is the clearest file-level public lane found in this pass. It publishes selected CNRS-scope reports or recommendations and states that such reports are not meant to remain online indefinitely.
- Sorbonne Universite annual report: the 2025 report is a representative institutional annual-report lane, showing local RIS, committee, ambassador-network and signalement activity without creating a decision archive.

### Coding refinements from France

- Separate handler directories from case outputs. A live national RIS directory is very valuable, but it answers "who handles" rather than "what happened in files."
- Separate national aggregate monitoring from case-file publication. Ofis gives national visibility into volumes and patterns, not reasoned decisions.
- Code operator case-report pages as selective micro-publication nodes. CNRS/MIS reports are real case material, but only for selected CNRS-scope outputs.
- Record retention or request language where official pages provide it. CNRS/MIS is important because publication is selective and not necessarily permanent.
- Keep institutional annual reports visible as a recurring transparency corridor, but mark whether they contain counts, narrative learning, signalement activity or actual decisions.
- Treat Inadis as a second-look route until its own criteria, procedure and output practice can be verified.

### Strongest public case-material sources

- Ofis synthesis 2022-2023: aggregate national monitoring.
- CNRS/MIS reports page: selective operator case-file lane.
- Sorbonne Universite 2025 integrity annual report: institutional annual-report lane.
- Ofis RIS directory: handler-map source, not case-output publication.
- Ofis July 2025 recommendations: procedure and Inadis-route source, not a case archive.

### Gaps for next pass

- Confirm whether Inadis has a stand-alone page, published procedure, criteria or public outputs after installation.
- Build a wider institutional annual-report sample beyond Sorbonne Universite.
- Check whether other operators publish selected reports, recommendations, annual statistics or only procedure pages.
- Revisit the CNRS/MIS reports page periodically because its own wording implies limited permanence and request-based access for older material.
- Keep health, animal, data-protection and bioethics registers separate from general research-misconduct case-file publication.

## Quality-consolidation pass added 29 April 2026

### Current-source check

The 29 April 2026 pass did not change the France system classification. France remains a legally codified, institution-first RIS network with Hceres/Ofis as the national coordination and observatory center rather than a national misconduct tribunal.

Current-source findings:

- The core Code de la recherche and decree baseline remains the right legal spine: L.211-2, D.211-2, D.211-3, D.211-4, L.114-3-1, Decree 2021-1536 and Decree 2021-1572.
- The Ofis surveys page still exposes the 2022-2023 synthesis as the main national aggregate handling source. It states that the synthesis was built from data transmitted by higher-education and research institutions in 2024.
- The Ofis 21 July 2025 recommendations remain the main current public procedure source for treatment of signalements and for the Inadis referral context.
- The Ofis 14 April 2026 "bilan et perspectives" item confirms that Ofis is actively reviewing the 2021-2026 integrity landscape and regulatory prospects; it is a current-source signal, not a case-output source.
- The CNRS/MIS reports page remains the strongest file-level public lane found for France. Its retention and request wording is analytically important because selected reports are not a permanent national archive.
- Sorbonne Universite remains the strongest located institutional annual-report corridor. Paris Cite and Toulouse add route-directory seeds, but the wider France pass should still test whether those and other institutions expose public annual reports, statistics or only procedure/contact pages.

### ENRIO cross-check

ENRIO has a France country report with a last-update note of May 2024. It confirms the basic reading used in the app: Ofis is a national structure, but it has no investigatory or decision-making authority; institutions remain responsible for handling cases through local RIS routes. ENRIO also confirms the 2015 national charter, the 2020 legal codification, the Ofis observatory and directory functions, and the local procedure model.

Use the ENRIO report as a secondary comparison source only. Its terminology and counts should not replace current primary-source checks, especially where Ofis pages or the live app note use newer source dates. The ENRIO 2024 annual report lists the French Office for Research Integrity (OFIS), France, as an ENRIO member.

### Main code and procedure baseline

Use this hierarchy for future France extraction:

- Code de la recherche L.211-2, D.211-2, D.211-3, D.211-4 and L.114-3-1 for the hard-law integrity, reporting, RIS and Hceres/Ofis baseline.
- Decree 2021-1572 and the 2017 MESR circular for institutional and RIS implementation.
- French Charter of Deontology for Research Professions, 2015, plus Ofis reference-text pages for the formative national code layer.
- ALLEA European Code of Conduct as a European reference cited in the French ecosystem, not as a substitute for French law.
- Ofis July 2025 recommendations and the later Ofis synthetic/new-feature sheets for treatment of signalements, dessaisissement and Inadis.

### Directory and repository indexing targets

The next France pass should index the following lanes separately:

- Ofis RIS annuaire: institution, named RIS, institution type if available, source date and linked institutional route.

- Paris Cite, Toulouse, Paris-Saclay, Bordeaux, Sciences Po and other institutional routes: distinguish committee/procedure/contact visibility from annual-report or case-output visibility.
- Inadis: watch for a stand-alone official page, criteria, published procedure, annual statistics, opinions or anonymized outputs.

### Stable conclusions

- Do not recode France as a single national misconduct-board system.
- Do not treat the Ofis RIS annuaire as case-publication evidence.
- Do not treat Inadis as an operating public archive unless future sources show outputs.
- Keep CNRS/MIS selected reports, Sorbonne annual reports and Ofis national synthesis as three separate transparency lanes.
- Keep CPP/CNRIPH, CCNE, CNREEA/CNEA, CNIL/DPO, deontology and research-security material in boundary layers unless a source explicitly routes the matter into RIS misconduct handling.
