# Montenegro deep-dive sources

Updated: 22 April 2026

## System reading

Montenegro is best read as a dual legal integrity model rather than a single national misconduct-board system.

- The research lane runs through the Ministry of Education, Science and Innovation, the 2025 Law on Scientific Research Activity, the strategy, the council, licensing rules and the register of licensed scientific research institutions.
- The academic-integrity lane runs through the 2019 Law on Academic Integrity, the 2025 Law on Higher Education, the national Ethics Committee and mandatory institutional ethics committees.
- AKOKVO is the bridge between those lanes because Standard 11 turns academic integrity into a reaccreditation requirement with a specific evidence list.
- The national Ethics Committee is real and active, with current reports, meeting notes and decisions, but most practical first-line handling still appears to begin institutionally.
- University of Montenegro and Mediterranean University provide the clearest institutional public routes in this pass.
- Clinical trials, medical devices, animal welfare, personal data and copyright or IP stay as specialist boundary regimes and should not be merged into the core academic-integrity chain.

## Key conclusions for coding

1. Code Montenegro as a split research-governance and academic-integrity system, not as a single national complaints board.
2. Keep the 2025 research-law lane separate from the 2019 academic-integrity law lane.
3. Treat the national Ethics Committee as a real, visible national body, but not as the sole handler of all integrity questions.
4. Keep AKOKVO visible because Standard 11 makes integrity a formal reaccreditation issue.
5. Treat University of Montenegro as the strongest public institutional example, but keep the model sector-wide by including Mediterranean University.
6. Keep clinical, animal, data and copyright or IP issues separate as boundary regimes.

## Public-transparency reading

No stable searchable national archive of academic-integrity findings was identified in this pass.

Public visibility is strongest for:

- laws and strategic framework pages
- Ethics Committee members, rules, annual reports and meeting or decision posts
- AKOKVO standards and reaccreditation rules
- university codes, rulebooks and reports
- specialist regulator pages

This means Montenegro is more transparent than a pure no-publication model, but still relies on distributed posting of reports, rules and occasional decision notices rather than a consolidated case archive.

## Committee and case-file pass added 23 April 2026

### National publication reading

- The national Ethics Committee should now be coded as a recurring publication stream, not as a few isolated news items.
- The current committee page on `gov.me` functions as a public hub for reports, session posts and some decision pages.
- Public continuity is stronger than the earlier draft suggested because there is also an archived 2020 decisions page.
- The annual reports for 2024-2025 state that `etickikomitet.edu.me` was used as a continuity site for current and previous committee terms; they also note cyber-attack-related instability, so this should be treated as an archive-continuity clue rather than as a currently verified live database.
- The strongest current national case-output page found in this pass is the VI session page, which reports a finding of autoplagiarism and links downloadable decision material.

### Institutional publication reading

- University of Montenegro now has clearer publication duties than the earlier draft captured: its 2025 rulebook requires publication of annual reports and decisions, and the university has a visible Ethics Board page.
- Mediterranean University's 2022 Ethics Code says the Ethics Board issues public statements on imposed measures and can require publication in the University Bulletin or in the media.
- University of Donja Gorica adds a third institutional model: its Ethics Code requires annual reports to the Senate, records of imposed measures and website publication of at least some sanctions.
- No stable live institutional decision archive was identified at UCG, Mediterranean University or UDG in this pass, but the local publication duties are explicit enough that they should be stored as part of the case-file workflow.

### Method refinement from Montenegro

- Recurring committee pages with session posts, reports and linked decisions count as a real publication stream even when they are not database-like.
- Archived predecessor pages should be coded separately from current committee hubs, because they can prove continuity without proving a complete live archive.
- Annual reports can reveal both continuity tools and referral patterns, including when the committee declares itself non-competent or receives matters from institutional bodies.
- Institutional codes and rulebooks that require annual reports, public statements, Bulletin publication, media publication or website publication should be recorded even when live outputs are still missing.
- Montenegro should be coded as stronger than current-news-only publication, but weaker than a searchable all-cases archive.
