# Malta deep-dive sources

Access date: 28 April 2026

## Initial app profile and committee/case-file seed pass

Malta has no local overview document in this folder. This pass therefore builds a
first app dossier from official public sources and should be treated as a seed
profile rather than a completed committee-and-case-file pilot.

## System reading

The current evidence supports a compact, distributed model:

- The Malta Further and Higher Education Authority (MFHEA) regulates licensing,
  accreditation, external quality assurance and provider audit. Its reports and
  provider accreditation outcome decisions are public, but this is a quality
  assurance corridor rather than a general research-misconduct archive.
- Xjenza Malta is the public R&I policy and funding agency. It manages national
  funding streams, policy/strategy support and Horizon Europe national-contact
  functions. Current funding rules expose administrative compliance, audit,
  confidentiality and data-protection controls rather than a public misconduct
  case route.
- University of Malta research integrity is visible through the Research Code of
  Practice, University Research Ethics Committee (UREC), Faculty Research Ethics
  Committees, UREC-DP and the URECA research ethics and data-protection platform.
- MCAST has an institutional Research Ethics Policy and Procedure, with Institute
  Research Committees, a Research Ethics Committee, a Research Ethics Appeals
  Committee and links to the Corporate Research Committee and disciplinary
  structures.
- Health Ethics Committee, Medicines Authority, animal-welfare, data-protection,
  IP and open-science sources are boundary regimes and should not be used as a
  substitute for general misconduct publication.

## Seed handling routes

### MFHEA quality-assurance corridor

MFHEA publishes external quality assurance audit reports, provider accreditation
outcome decisions and follow-up files. These are useful for institutional
quality, governance, accountability and programme/provider compliance, but they
are not research-misconduct case files unless a specific report is later found
to contain misconduct handling.

### University of Malta UREC/FREC/UREC-DP route

The University of Malta's Research Code of Practice applies to staff, students,
visiting or affiliate staff, associates, contractors and consultants undertaking
research on University premises, using its facilities or on its behalf. The
Research Ethics Review Procedures route research ethics and data-protection
self-assessments through Faculty Research Ethics Committees, with escalation to
UREC-E or UREC-DP depending on the issue. UREC-DP liaises with the Information
and Data Protection Commissioner for special-category data matters. The FAQ
states that failure to follow the Code and procedures is a serious breach of
University regulations and may lead to disciplinary measures, but no public
case-output page was located in this pass.

### MCAST REC/IRC/REAC route

The MCAST Research Ethics Policy and Procedure sets up Institute Research
Committees, a Research Ethics Committee, and a Research Ethics Appeals Committee.
The REC reviews staff, student and external research proposals, promotes good
research practice, samples Institute-level decisions and reports minutes to the
Corporate Research Committee. The REAC handles appeals against REC decisions.
This is a clear internal route, but no public decision archive or annual case
statistics were located.

### Xjenza Malta funder route

Xjenza Malta is the public R&I policy and funding agency. Its programme pages and
rules provide the grant-management route, including submission, eligibility,
contract, audit, confidentiality and data-protection controls. They do not yet
provide a public research-misconduct decision archive.

### Health, clinical and data routes

The Health Ethics Committee reviews health research, clinical-trial and
medical-device/in-vitro-diagnostic proposals referred to it. The Medicines
Authority handles clinical-trial notification, licensing authority functions and
GCP inspections, including complaint-triggered risk assessment. These routes are
health-research boundary routes, not general research-misconduct publication.

## Public case-material finding

No national research-misconduct decision archive, institutional UREC/FREC case
archive, MCAST REC decision archive or Xjenza Malta misconduct-decision archive
was located in this pass.

Positive but limited public material:

- MFHEA public audit reports and provider accreditation outcome decisions:
  quality-assurance corridor.
- University of Malta research-ethics FAQs: procedure and audit consequences,
  including possible disciplinary measures.
- MCAST research-ethics policy: internal review, appeal and reporting route.
- University of Malta SST journal ethics page: a journal-level publication
  ethics route for errors, retractions and misconduct allegations, not a national
  or institutional misconduct archive.
- Health Ethics Committee and Medicines Authority pages: clinical/health
  research approval and inspection corridors.
- IDPC annual and public-body material: data-protection supervision.
