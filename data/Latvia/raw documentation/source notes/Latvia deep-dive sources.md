# Latvia deep-dive sources

Updated: 24 April 2026

## Committee-and-case-file pass

This pass started from `Overview Latvia.docx` and the existing Latvia app dossier. The source-notes folder did not contain a prior deep-dive note file, so this file records the first committee-and-case-file sweep.

Latvia should still be read as a distributed, code-led and institutionally executed system, not a Dutch/LOWI-style national case board. The national layer is real: Satversme, the Law on Scientific Activity, the Latvian Council of Science, the Latvian Academy of Sciences and the Code of Ethics for Scientists create the main public vocabulary and the LAS/LCS Scientists' Ethics Committee route. The pass did not locate a current national LAS/LCS case-output archive.

The most important correction is at institutional level. The University of Latvia Latvian-language Academic Ethics Commission page publishes a public decision list by year. It includes decisions from 2019 through 2026 and at least one research-authorship case: the 7 January 2025 decision on a co-authorship breach. That decision is anonymized, finds an academic-ethics breach, recommends publication-level action, and points the applicants toward the Latvian Young Scientists Association Ethics Commission and the LAS/LCS Ethics Commission. This is a case-file fragment, but it is institutional and academic-ethics scoped, not national.

The University of Latvia research-ethics pages and opinion registers are a separate visibility lane. They are useful for approvals and ethics opinions, but they should not be merged with misconduct or academic-ethics case findings. RSU, RTU and LBTU expose procedure, policy, research-ethics or academic-integrity routes, but this pass did not locate comparable public decision repositories for them.

Biomedical, clinical, genome, animal, data-protection, open-science and AI routes remain boundary regimes. The Central Medical Ethics Committee page is current and gives meeting/application information, but it is a biomedical ethics approval route rather than a general research-misconduct publication body.

## Coding conclusion

Code Latvia as fragmented institutional case visibility. The University of Latvia decision list justifies moving Latvia above procedure-only visibility, but it does not create a national archive. The LAS/LCS route remains a national norm and possible referral route until current public outputs are located.
