# Latvia deep-dive sources

Updated: 30 April 2026

## Committee-and-case-file pass

This pass started from `Overview Latvia.docx` and the existing Latvia app dossier. The source-notes folder did not contain a prior deep-dive note file, so this file records the first committee-and-case-file sweep.

Latvia should still be read as a distributed, code-led and institutionally executed system, not a Dutch/LOWI-style national case board. The national layer is real: Satversme, the Law on Scientific Activity, the Latvian Council of Science, the Latvian Academy of Sciences and the Code of Ethics for Scientists create the main public vocabulary and the LAS/LCS Scientists' Ethics Committee route. The pass did not locate a current national LAS/LCS case-output archive.

The most important correction is at institutional level. The University of Latvia Latvian-language Academic Ethics Commission page publishes a public decision list by year. It includes decisions from 2019 through 2026 and at least one research-authorship case: the 7 January 2025 decision on a co-authorship breach. That decision is anonymized, finds an academic-ethics breach, recommends publication-level action, and points the applicants toward the Latvian Young Scientists Association Ethics Commission and the LAS/LCS Ethics Commission. This is a case-file fragment, but it is institutional and academic-ethics scoped, not national.

The University of Latvia research-ethics pages and opinion registers are a separate visibility lane. They are useful for approvals and ethics opinions, but they should not be merged with misconduct or academic-ethics case findings. RSU, RTU and LBTU expose procedure, policy, research-ethics or academic-integrity routes, but this pass did not locate comparable public decision repositories for them.

Biomedical, clinical, genome, animal, data-protection, open-science and AI routes remain boundary regimes. The Central Medical Ethics Committee page is current and gives meeting/application information, but it is a biomedical ethics approval route rather than a general research-misconduct publication body.

## Quality-consolidation pass

Updated 29 April 2026.

The current-source check confirms the 24 April coding. Latvia still has no single national misconduct-board route comparable to LOWI, TENK or DBRM. ENRIO's country-report archive does not list Latvia among the updated country reports, and the 2024 ENRIO annual report lists no Latvian member organisation. Use that as comparison evidence only: it supports the conclusion that Latvia is outside the main ENRIO national-office reporting set, but it does not prove absence of domestic institutional routes.

The national code baseline remains the 1997/2017 Code of Ethics for Scientists, owned by LAS/LCS, plus LZA statute wording on developing and maintaining the scientist ethics code and scientific-discussion principles. Current LCS pages strengthen the funder and expert-governance layer: LCS functions include developing ethical norms, project/expert evaluation, conflict prevention and report publication; LCS expert commissions and the Scientific Expertise Commission are public route directories, but they are not misconduct case archives.

Representative UL case-level examples:

- 28 May 2019 plagiarism/whistleblowing-routing decision: the commission said the whistleblowing-law route would not be used, recognized the need to evaluate public plagiarism allegations, and stated that plagiarism signs in teaching and scientific works fall to a rector-appointed expert commission, with the rector or delegate making the final decision.
- 28 June 2024 authorship/plagiarism decision: the commission found a serious academic-integrity breach in a medical-faculty lecturer's book due to incomplete citation practice, while recording no initial malicious intent and recommending correction cooperation.
- 28 June 2024 Turnitin/student-work decision: the commission found no professor breach in escalating a master's-thesis similarity issue, but recommended another expert or working group and review of plagiarism-control and AI-use rules.
- 7 January 2025 co-authorship decision: the commission found an authorship/co-authorship breach connected to a scientific article, recommended publication-level correction/removal steps, and pointed the applicants toward the Latvian Young Scientists Association Ethics Commission and the LAS/LCS Ethics Commission.
- 2 March 2026 respectful-communication decision: useful as a currency marker for the decision stream, but not research-misconduct evidence.

Wider institutional route checks:

- RSU now has a direct research-integrity page tied to the ALLEA European Code, plus a 21 February 2023 Academic Integrity Policy. The policy applies academic and research integrity to students, academic and administrative staff, creates ad hoc Academic Integrity Panels for complex cases, routes possible academic/administrative staff fraud or academic-integrity breaches to the RSU Ethics Committee, and keeps the RSU Research Ethics Committee as a separate medical/biomedical approval route.
- RISEBA has a research-integrity page applying the 2023 ALLEA European Code and states that concerns or breaches are evaluated and resolved by the RISEBA Ethics Board. Its documents page links the Code of Ethics, Code of Academic Integrity, European Code and generative-AI guidance. No decision archive was found.
- Daugavpils University's Journal of Comparative Studies publication-ethics page points to a Humanities and Social Sciences Research Ethics Committee regulation based on the Law on Scientific Activity, the LAS/LCS scientist ethics code, the university code and DU Satversme. This is a useful institutional route seed and publication-ethics trace, not a public decision repository.
- Vidzeme University of Applied Sciences publishes documents and an English Ethics Regulation. The regulation defines research-ethics violations including data falsification, tendentious analysis, participant harms, plagiarism/self-plagiarism and use of others' data without attribution; it routes sanctions and appeals through an Academic Ethics Commission and Academic Court of Arbitration. It appears to be procedure-only public visibility.

### LAS/LCS retest and public-research-institute widening follow-up

Updated 30 April 2026.

This is the next quality check chosen from the quality-plan order because Latvia already had a full UL decision classification but still needed LAS/LCS output retesting and a wider public-research-institute sample.

National LAS/LCS retest:

- LCS now has a direct `Ētika` page. It was published on 3 February 2023 and links the LCS Ethics Code added on 6 February 2026 and the Scientist's Ethics Code added on 9 February 2026. This improves source currency for the national code baseline.
- The LCS Ethics Code is an internal public-administration staff code for LCS employees. It creates an LCS employee Ethics Commission route for staff conduct complaints, but it is not the joint LAS/LCS Scientist's Ethics Committee named in the national scientist code.

This pass still did not locate a current public page, composition list, decision archive, opinion list or annual reporting stream for that joint scientist committee.
- LCS expert commissions and the Scientific Expertise Commission remain current public route directories for expert-rights and project-governance matters. They expose scientific-ethics obligations for experts, but no misconduct case-output repository was found.

## Coding conclusion

Code Latvia as fragmented institutional case visibility. The University of Latvia decision list justifies moving Latvia above procedure-only visibility, but it does not create a national archive or a dedicated research-misconduct database. The 29 April 2026 quality pass added a quantified and title-level row-indexed UL decision stream, ENRIO comparison, wider institutional route seeds and a funder matrix; the 30 April 2026 follow-up full-text classified all 78 UL decision pages and confirms that only a small subset is core research/publication integrity or research-project governance. The 30 April 2026 LAS/LCS and public-research-institute follow-up improves the route directory through LCS, ISSP UL, LIOS, EDI, LVKĶI and BMC sources, but it does not locate a national or institute-level public decision archive beyond UL.
