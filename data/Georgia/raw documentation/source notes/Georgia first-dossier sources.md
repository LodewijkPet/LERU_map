# Georgia first expanded dossier sources

Accessed: 28 April 2026

## System reading

Georgia is best read as a distributed science-governance, QA and institutional research-integrity system rather than a single national research-misconduct board.

The national science-law backbone is visible through Matsne: the Law on Science, Technology and Their Development, the Law on Higher Education and the Law on the Georgian National Academy of Sciences. The Academy route is especially important for annual scientific-report assessment and completed scientific-research work assessment, but this is evaluation/advisory accountability rather than a misconduct case archive.

The clearest current research-integrity signal is the Erasmus+ ETHICS project, coordinated by Ilia State University and involving Georgian HEIs plus the Ministry, NCEQE and SRNSFG. The project explicitly targets doctoral accreditation policy, research-integrity modules/MOOCs, research support/integrity officers, university policies, research integrity/ethics boards, support services and responsible science communication.

NCEQE provides the strongest public QA file corridor through authorization, accreditation, monitoring, follow-up and appeal decisions, annual reports and the HEI register. These sources should be coded as QA and not as general research-misconduct decisions unless a specific file says otherwise.

Institutional ethics and integrity routes are visible at TSU, TSMU, Ilia, East European University and Caucasus International University. The public material is mostly procedure, policy, committee composition and review-route evidence; no public institutional misconduct case archive was found in this first pass.

Biomedical, clinical, data-protection, IP, animal-research and journal-publication routes are boundary lanes.
