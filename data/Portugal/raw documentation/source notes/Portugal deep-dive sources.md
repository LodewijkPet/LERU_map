# Portugal deep-dive sources

Updated: 23 April 2026

## System reading

Portugal is best read as a distributed research-integrity system with one stronger regulated clinical-research ethics network.

- FCT is the national funder and policy anchor. It shapes conduct through funding rules, internal ethics and compliance material, open science, and research-data infrastructure.
- CNECV is the clearest national advisory and agenda-setting source on scientific integrity, especially Recommendation 1/2018.
- General research-integrity handling appears mainly institutional. No LOWI-like national board for general cases was identified in this pass.
- CEIC and the RNCES health-ethics structure form the clearest formal national-local committee network, but that lane should stay separate from general misconduct handling.
- Coimbra, Porto, Minho, UCP and CES together show the diversity of Portuguese local routes: ethics committees, codes, public guidance, Provedoria and specialized subcommittees.
- Open access, Re.Data, CNPD and DGAV are important prevention or boundary actors, not substitutes for misconduct procedures.

## Key conclusions for coding

1. Portugal should be coded as a distributed system anchored in FCT, CNECV and local institutional routes, not as a centralized general misconduct-board system.
2. CNECV Recommendation 1/2018 is crucial because it shows both the national concern and the absence of a mature pre-existing general national committee.
3. CEIC and RNCES form the clearest formal national-local committee network, but they belong to the clinical and biomedical ethics lane rather than the whole integrity system.
4. The institution layer carries most general execution through committees, codes, ombuds or Provedoria roles, data offices, plagiarism tools and local procedures.
5. FCT's open-science and research-data infrastructure should remain visible as prevention architecture rather than case handling.
6. Portugal's public visibility is distributed and route-based, not archive-based.

## Public-transparency reading

Portugal is not a true archive system and no national searchable archive of general research-integrity decisions was identified in this pass.

Public visibility is strongest for:

- laws and advisory texts
- CEIC and health-ethics route pages
- institutional committee regulations and ethics portals
- some local opinions, recommendations and ombuds routes
- funder, open-science and data-governance policy pages

The most accurate transparency reading is therefore:

- stronger than a pure no-publication model because the route architecture, committee rules and some local outputs are visible
- weaker than a summary-reporting or archive model because there is no recurring national public case reporting for general research integrity

## Committee-and-case-file pass added 23 April 2026

This pilot materially upgrades Portugal from a pure route-visibility reading.

- U.Porto's CEUP page states that its work results in documents, opinions and recommendations available in SIGARRA.
- The linked public documents page exposes folders for approved opinions, own-initiative opinions and institutional-request opinions.
- Coimbra's CEIUC 2024 activity report shows local aggregate reporting on meetings, requests and opinion handling.
- CEIC publishes yearly indicators and a public `Pareceres em Avaliacao` status page, but this is a clinical or biomedical boundary lane and should not be merged into general misconduct publication.
- Portugal should therefore be coded as having institutional opinion archives and local aggregate reporting without a national general-integrity archive.
