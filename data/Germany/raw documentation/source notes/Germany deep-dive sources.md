# Germany deep-dive sources

Updated: 23 April 2026

## System reading

Germany is not a single national misconduct-board system.

The strongest national baseline is:

- Article 5(3) of the Basic Law
- the DFG Code of Conduct in force since 1 August 2019
- the DFG funding rule that institutions must implement the code in legally binding form
- the implementation note that this local binding form must rest on sufficient Land-law foundations

That baseline is then operationalized through a distributed network:

- OWID as the confidential nationwide ombuds and mediation layer
- DFG's own inquiry and sanction route for DFG-governed cases
- university and university-medical-center ombudspersons, offices and ethics routes
- organization-wide systems in Helmholtz, Leibniz, Max Planck and Fraunhofer
- adjacent specialist regimes for clinical trials, medical devices, animal research, data protection, research data, security-relevant research and export control

## Key conclusions for coding

1. Code Germany as a distributed, federal and strongly self-regulatory system, not as a single national tribunal.
2. Treat DFG as the practical national minimum because DFG funding eligibility forces legally binding local implementation of the code.
3. Treat OWID as a national confidential advice and mediation layer, not as a sanctioning appeals court.
4. Treat the DFG Committee of Inquiry and Joint Committee as the clearest national sanction route, but only within DFG jurisdiction.
5. Keep university, university-medical-center and major research-organization routes visible because much of the actual case handling remains local or organization-specific.
6. Keep clinical, animal, data and security rules as adjacent boundary regimes rather than folding them into one misconduct system.

## Public-transparency reading

Germany has no single searchable national archive for research-misconduct decisions across the whole system.

The clearest public visibility in this pass came from:

- OWID annual reports with statistics and anonymized summaries
- DFG procedure pages and selected DFG press releases on sanctions
- university and organization-level ombuds and ethics pages

## Remaining gaps for a final sweep

- More explicit current Land-level implementation examples behind the DFG legal-binding requirement
- Stronger institute-level public routes for Fraunhofer and Max Planck
- A cleaner research-specific BfDI and Land data-protection trail
- More university medical center examples beyond Charite
- Any public case publication beyond DFG press releases and OWID summary reporting

## Committee and case-file pass added 23 April 2026

### Main case-publication finding

Germany has a public case-publication ladder rather than a single national misconduct database.

The strongest stable case-publication lane found in this pass is the DFG page listing scientific-misconduct press releases from the last ten years. It is durable and case-level, but it is limited to DFG jurisdiction. It should not be coded as a comprehensive German archive.

### Route classes

#### DFG press-release list

- Type: funder case-publication list
- Lane: DFG-governed applications, funded work, reviewers and committee work
- Strongest source: Research Integrity portal list of DFG scientific-misconduct press releases
- Publication owner: DFG / Research Integrity portal
- Publication depth: individual or grouped public sanction notices
- Publicity mode: stable yearly list plus individual press-release pages
- Case-file status: strongest public case-publication lane found, but not national-comprehensive

Representative current examples:

- DFG press release 08/2026: anonymized idea-theft case, written reprimand and two-year DFG proposal ban.
- DFG press release 20/2025: named image-manipulation case, written reprimands and one-year proposal ban for one respondent.
- DFG press release 23/2025: anonymized Habilitation plagiarism case, written reprimand and three-year proposal/reviewer ban.

#### DFG confidential intake

- Type: confidential intake route
- Lane: reports of potential DFG-related misconduct
- Strongest source: DFG misconduct page plus BKMS incident reporting platform
- Publication owner: DFG
- Publication depth: intake and reporting mechanism only
- Publicity mode: public reporting route, confidential handling
- Case-file status: not a public database

#### OWID annual reporting

- Type: national ombuds annual reporting
- Lane: nationwide confidential advice and mediation
- Strongest sources: OWID annual reports page and annual report 2024
- Publication owner: OWID
- Publication depth: aggregate statistics, anonymized summaries and procedural reflection
- Publicity mode: recurring public reports
- Case-file status: aggregate reporting, not individual files

#### Institutional annual-report routes

- Type: local annual reporting
- Lane: university ombuds, coordination office and investigation-commission routes
- Strongest sources in this pass: Freie Universitat Berlin GWP annual report 2025 and Gottingen Ombudsstelle annual report 2022
- Publication owner: individual institution
- Publication depth: local aggregate tables or narrative category-level summaries
- Publicity mode: institutional PDF reports
- Case-file status: annual-report statistics and summaries, not full decisions

Freie Universitat Berlin is the strongest local reporting example found in this pass. Its 2025 GWP annual report gives tables by suspected misconduct category, faculty and career stage, including a total of 58 preliminary reviews or advice conversations. This is highly useful for coding institutional transparency depth, but it is still not a case-file archive.

#### Shared Berlin ombuds route

- Type: shared institutional handling route
- Lane: Berlin higher-education institutions
- Strongest source: FU Berlin 2025 press release on the shared ombuds office
- Publication owner: participating Berlin institutions / FU Berlin as initial host
- Publication depth: route description
- Publicity mode: public announcement
- Case-file status: handling route, not publication route

The shared Berlin route matters because it can examine suspected misconduct when an institution cannot do so itself for understandable reasons. It should be stored as a shared institutional route. Degree-withdrawal procedures remain with the individual higher education institutions.

### Method refinement from Germany

The Germany pass adds five rules for countries with strong funder self-regulation and confidential ombuds layers:

- Treat a curated funder press-release list as a case-publication lane, but record its jurisdiction limits before assigning a country-level transparency tier.
- Separate confidential intake portals from public databases. Intake proves where allegations enter; it does not prove publication.
- Code national ombuds reports as aggregate transparency unless they publish individual decisions.
- Search for institutional annual reports, because local universities may publish richer tables than the national ombuds body.
- Store shared or cross-institutional ombuds offices as handling infrastructure even when they do not publish case files.
