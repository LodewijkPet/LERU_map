# Switzerland deep-dive sources

Updated: 24 April 2026

## System reading

Switzerland is a federal and institutionally executed research-integrity system with a shared national code, a new national monitoring/advisory body, real funder-side sanction routes, varied institutional procedures and strong separate human-research and animal-research boundary regimes.

- SCCSI opened on 1 January 2026 as a national reporting, advisory and monitoring body.
- SCCSI is not a national misconduct tribunal: it does not conduct proceedings, advise on the merits of individual cases or hear appeals.
- SNSF is the strongest current public case-material lane because it publishes annual anonymised reports and occasional case news.
- ETH Zurich has one of the strongest institutional publication rules found in this pass: anonymised investigation reports are normally published after concluded proceedings, and non-public reports are to be covered in the public annual report.
- A live ETH Zurich institutional list of anonymised investigation reports was located on the scientific-misconduct page. The list is rendered through an ETH table JSON endpoint and currently exposes four downloadable reports: UV 12.05.2025-1, UV 12.05.2025-2, UV 10.12.2025-1 and UV 10.12.2025-2.
- RAPS/HumRes, Swissmedic and animal-experiment routes are strong public or semi-public boundary systems, not general misconduct decision archives.

## Committee and case-file pass

Date: 24 April 2026

### Seed bodies

- Body: SCCSI
- Type: National reporting, advisory and monitoring body
- Lane: National scientific-integrity monitoring and institutional support
- Role: Receives annual reports from higher-education institutions without personal details, monitors proceedings/sanctions/measures, advises institutions on procedure, refers individuals to appropriate institutional contact points and prepares annual public information.
- Strongest current source: SCCSI mandate page.
- Publication owner: SCCSI / Swiss Academies.
- Archive scope: No current case archive. Future annual reports are expected to summarize monitoring without personal details.
- Database function: National monitoring and referral hub, not a misconduct decision archive.
- Individual case visibility: None at SCCSI level in this pass.
- Why this is or is not a misconduct case file: SCCSI explicitly does not conduct proceedings, advise on merits or act as appeal body.
- Publication basis: Mandate requires annual report and public information.

- Body: SNSF Commission on Research Integrity and Plagiarism Control Group
- Type: National funder case-handling route
- Lane: SNSF applications, grants, publication grants and funder-funded work
- Role: Investigates suspected scientific misconduct inside SNSF competence; employer/institution remains primarily responsible for institutional misconduct; Academic Board decides sanctions under the 2025 regulations.
- Strongest current source: SNSF Scientific Integrity page, 2025 Research Integrity Regulations and 2024 annual report.
- Publication owner: SNSF.
- Archive scope: Annual anonymised reports plus occasional case news; no searchable decision database.
- Database function: Annual-report corridor and selected news communication.
- Micro-publication node: 2 April 2024 plagiarism-sanctions news item.
- Individual case visibility: Specific public case news in selected cases; annual reports give anonymised statistics and short rows.
- Public data elements: Case counts, source of reports, random checks, detailed analyses, minor errors, Commission cases, sanctions count and broad outcome.
- Non-public or restricted elements: Full file, evidence, detailed reasoning, names except where public through external route, full commission report and appeal file.
- Why this is or is not a misconduct case file: The annual report is genuine case-material visibility but summary-level; the news item is case-specific but not a full decision report.
- Publication basis: SNSF page states annual anonymised committee reports; 2025 regulation requires anonymised annual report by the Investigation Group.

- Body: ETH Zurich Integrity Commission and Scientific Integrity Office
- Type: Institutional investigation and publication-rule route
- Lane: ETH Zurich suspected scientific misconduct
- Role: Scientific Integrity Office receives reports and triages with the Integrity Commission chair; Integrity Commission investigates and produces an investigation report; Executive Board Committee decides measures.
- Strongest current source: ETH Zurich 8 February 2024 news item and Scientific Misconduct page.
- Publication owner: ETH Zurich Scientific Integrity Office / ETH website.
- Archive scope: Live institutional list of anonymised investigation reports plus a separate aggregate procedure table. This is ETH Zurich only, not a Swiss national archive.
- Database function: Institutional case-report list and aggregate procedure table.
- Individual case visibility: Four anonymised reports located in the table: authorship/plagiarism, authorship, falsification and plagiarism. ETH also reports aggregate procedure counts for 1 June 2024-31 December 2024 and 1 January 2025-31 July 2025.
- Public data elements expected: Anonymised investigation report, case number, broad form of alleged misconduct, reasoning and measures, subject to publication timing and exceptions.
- Non-public or restricted elements: Names, full evidence, deliberations and reports withheld for cogent reasons.
- Why this is or is not a misconduct case file: This is a true institutional case-file fragment, closer to LOWI-style learning material, but its scope is limited to ETH Zurich and it should not be treated as a Swiss national archive.
- Publication basis: 2024 ETH procedure announcement and the live ETH scientific-misconduct table.

- Body: Innosuisse
- Type: Innovation funder route
- Lane: Innosuisse-funded scientific work and applications
- Role: Investigates alleged violations connected with funding or applications and can reject, reduce, terminate or exclude.
- Strongest current source: Innosuisse Scientific Integrity page.
- Publication owner: Innosuisse.
- Archive scope: Procedure and sanction-rule visibility; no public case output located.
- Why this is or is not a misconduct case file: A real funder sanction route exists, but no public case archive or anonymised case report was found.

- Body: Human research, clinical-trial and animal-experiment boundary routes
- Type: Boundary regimes
- Lane: Human research ethics, clinical trials, RAPS, Swissmedic, animal experiments, animex-ch and cantonal authorisations
- Role: Ethics approval, study/project registry, regulatory authorisation and animal-experiment licensing.
- Strongest current source: HumRes/FOPH about page, swissethics, RAPS help document and FSVO researchers page.
- Archive scope: RAPS is a public registry of approved ethics-committee projects since January 2016 and has declined-project statistics; this is not a general misconduct archive.
- Why this is or is not a misconduct case file: These sources publish approval and registry metadata, not findings on scientific misconduct.

## Quality-consolidation pass - 30 April 2026

Switzerland was selected for the next quality check because the existing committee-and-case-file pass was strong but source-sensitive: the ENRIO country report was last updated before SCCSI opened, while SCCSI became operational on 1 January 2026 and ETH Zurich has live client-side case tables.

### Source currency

- ENRIO's Switzerland country report remains useful comparison evidence, but it is last updated May 2024 and says there was then no national research-integrity body. That wording should now be read historically: SCCSI has since opened as a national reporting, advisory and monitoring body, while still not becoming a national tribunal or appeal body.
- SCCSI's home, mandate, legal-basis and Council pages are live current sources. The mandate page confirms annual reporting from higher-education institutions without personal details, monitoring of proceedings/sanctions/measures, procedural advice to institutions, referral of individuals to institutional contact points, annual reporting to the Higher Education Council and public information duties.
- The SCCSI Council page confirms the governance layer: Edwin Constable was elected first president effective 1 January 2025, and the vice-president plus members were elected on 27 November 2025 for four years from 1 January 2026. No SCCSI annual public report or case-output page was located in this pass.
- SNSF's scientific-integrity page is current through the 19 March 2025 Research Integrity Regulations and still links the 2024 Commission/Plagiarism Control Group report plus an older-report ZIP. No 2025 integrity annual report was located on 30 April 2026.
- ETH Zurich's two table endpoints still expose four anonymised investigation reports and two aggregate reporting periods. The anonymised report table lists UV 12.05.2025-1, UV 12.05.2025-2, UV 10.12.2025-1 and UV 10.12.2025-2; the aggregate table covers 1 June 2024-31 December 2024 and 1 January 2025-31 July 2025.
- Swiss Academies' scientific-integrity page now functions as both code source and route directory. It lists responsible ombuds and integrity-office bodies across universities, universities of applied sciences, teacher-education institutions and other higher-education institutions.
- swissuniversities' scientific-integrity page and 2024 annual report remain useful implementation sources: the page links the code, SCCSI and 2024 survey, while the annual report says a large majority of higher-education institutions reviewed or supplemented regulations and/or adopted the Code as a reference document.

### ENRIO status

- ENRIO has a Switzerland country report. The report confirms the SNSF Commission route, the Swiss Academies code role and the 2021 Swiss Code baseline, but it predates SCCSI's January 2026 opening. Use it as secondary comparison evidence, not as the final current-source statement.
- ENRIO's Switzerland member page lists two Swiss institutions: the Swiss National Science Foundation, ENRIO member since 2023, and the Swiss Academies of Arts and Sciences, ENRIO member since 2020. This strengthens the SNSF/Swiss Academies coding and avoids treating SCCSI as the only national-facing actor.

### Main code baseline

- Swiss national baseline: the 2021 Code of Conduct for Scientific Integrity remains the main Swiss code. It was prepared by Swiss Academies, SNSF, swissuniversities and Innosuisse, and it remains the basis named by SCCSI.
- European baseline: the ALLEA European Code of Conduct for Research Integrity, revised 2023, is the current European reference. It should be coded as a European and EU-funded-research baseline alongside, not instead of, the 2021 Swiss code.
- Funder baseline: SNSF now combines the 2021 Swiss code, the 2025 SNSF Research Integrity Regulations, RIPA article 12 and ALLEA 2023. Innosuisse also uses the 2021 code and lists misconduct categories including fabrication, falsification, plagiarism, authorship, publication lists, data/material handling, collaboration, expert reports and proceedings.

### Coding conclusion

The 30 April 2026 quality pass confirms the Swiss dossier but sharpens the date logic. Switzerland should no longer be summarized with the ENRIO report's pre-2026 "no national body" wording unless the caveat is explicit. SCCSI is now a live national monitoring/advisory/reporting body, but the transparency tier remains fragmented funder and institutional visibility because SCCSI does not conduct proceedings or hear appeals. The strongest public misconduct material remains SNSF annual anonymised reporting and ETH Zurich's four-report institutional table.
