### Working classification

Iceland is a statutory national-body system with weak public case-file visibility, not a bodyless or purely institutional system.

The strongest general research-integrity route is the Committee on Good Scientific Practice under Act No. 70/2019. The law gives the committee a national opinion route, institutional notification flow, dismissal/referral powers, and an annual-report duty. In this pass I did not locate a stable public archive of committee opinions, annual reports, procedure rules, or searchable misconduct case files.

The most visible public case-like material sits in boundary routes: health-research licences, hospital and primary-care research approvals, and IRF funding-compliance rules. These are important public accountability routes, but they should not be coded as general research-misconduct findings.

### Route directory

#### 1. Committee on Good Scientific Practice

- Body: Committee on Good Scientific Practice.
- Source: Act No. 70/2019 on good scientific practice (`https://www.althingi.is/lagas/nuna/2019070.html`).
- Role: national committee for recognised ethical standards in research and good scientific practice.
- Route: institutions and companies investigate suspected breaches locally and notify the national committee; institutions, interested parties, statutory ethics committees, or the committee itself can trigger a national opinion route.
- Public-output rule: the Act requires annual reports and says personal identifiers should be removed before publication where appropriate. It also requires procedure rules to be published on the committee website.
- Public-output gap: no public committee opinion archive, annual-report archive, or procedure-rule page was located in this pass.

#### 2. Legislative history and system rationale

- Source: bill and explanatory memorandum for Act No. 70/2019 (`https://www.althingi.is/altext/149/s/1239.html`).
- Role: explains that Iceland moved from self-regulation toward a central statutory committee, while keeping primary responsibility with researchers, institutions and companies.
- Useful signal: the memorandum frames the committee as a central actor that can advise, educate and handle referred or self-initiated matters, but expected case volume was not large.

#### 3. University and public higher-education route

- Sources: University of Iceland Science Committee page (`https://english.hi.is/research/innovation/want-work-innovation/science-committees`) and Code of Research Ethics for Public Higher Education Institutions (`https://english.hi.is/sites/default/files/2024-10/code_of_research_ethics_for_public_higher_education_institutions.pdf`).
- Role: non-health research-ethics review for lecturers, students, research specialists and other staff, especially where participants, children, vulnerable groups or identifiable data are involved.
- Public-output signal: the code says the Research Ethics Committee reports regularly on its operations on its website.
- Case-file gap: no public decision archive or individual opinion archive was located in this pass.

#### 4. University code-breach routes

- Main examples already in the dossier: University of Iceland Ethics Committee, Reykjavik University Ethics Committee, University of Akureyri code/regulations, Agricultural University of Iceland committees and Bifrost University Ethics Committee.
- Role: local handling of code breaches, plagiarism, participant protection, falsification, data distortion, IP and research conduct.
- Publication pattern: procedure and composition visibility is stronger than public outcome visibility.

#### 5. National Bioethics Committee

- Main source: National Bioethics Committee page (`https://island.is/en/o/national-bioethics-committee`).
- Public register source: issued licences page (`https://island.is/en/o/national-bioethics-committee/Issued-licenses-for-scientific-research-in-the-health-field`).
- Role: evaluates scientific research in the health field for scientific and ethical compatibility.
- Public-output signal: the issued-licences page provides a searchable overview of issued health-research licences.
- Limitation: the English page states that not all issued research licences are displayed and points to the Icelandic version for a more complete overview.
- Classification: boundary approval/register transparency, not general misconduct case-file publication.

#### 6. Landspitali and health-service institutional committees

- Sources: Landspitali scientific research page (`https://island.is/en/o/landspitali/scientific-research`), scientific research committees and boards page (`https://island.is/en/o/landspitali/scientific-research-committees-and-boards`), Landspitali IRB page (`https://island.is/en/o/landspitali/institutional-review-board-of-landspitali`), and Primary Health Care HH Science Committee page (`https://island.is/en/o/hh/scientific-research/hh-science-committee`).
- Role: hospital and primary-care research approval and oversight routes.
- Strongest public detail:
  - Landspitali lists the IRB, Administrative Research Ethics Committee, Scientific Council, Medical Research Review Committee and Medicines Committee.
  - Landspitali research using patient records or patient data needs approvals through the medical research review route, Chief Medical Officer where relevant, National Bioethics Committee for drug studies or external collaborations, and the hospital health-research ethics route.
  - The HH Science Committee reviews scientific research at Primary Health Care of the Capital Area, evaluates ethical considerations and may reject a project even if the national bioethics committee approved it.
- Classification: health-research approval visibility, not misconduct case files.

#### 7. Rannis / Icelandic Research Fund

- Sources: IRF page (`https://en.rannis.is/funding/research/icelandic-research-fund/`), IRF Handbook for grant year 2026 (`https://en.rannis.is/media/rannsoknasjodur/IRF_Handbook.pdf`), and IRF final and annual reports page (`https://en.rannis.is/funding/research/icelandic-research-fund/final--annual-reports/`).
- Role: national funder route for permits, approvals, data management, conflicts of interest, reporting, open access and misconduct consequences.
- Strongest misconduct clause: the 2026 handbook says suspicion of research misconduct, fabrication, falsification, plagiarism or misappropriation in an application or funded project triggers notification to the institution and IRF Board; review-phase suspicions can withdraw the application; the IRF Board can initiate an independent investigation.
- Public-output pattern: funder procedure and reporting obligations are public; no public IRF misconduct decision archive was located.

#### 8. Data-protection and IP boundary

- Sources already in the dossier: Data Protection Authority pages, Act No. 90/2018 and UI/Landspitali IP Committee.
- Role: personal data, health-data transfer, data reuse, employee inventions and research-result commercialisation.
- Classification: important boundary lane; do not treat privacy or IP handling as research-misconduct findings unless a source explicitly connects them.

### Case-file and publication assessment

- No public general research-misconduct decision archive was found.
- No public archive of Committee on Good Scientific Practice opinions was found.
- No public annual-report archive for the Committee on Good Scientific Practice was found, despite the statutory annual-report duty.
- The National Bioethics Committee issued-licences page is public and searchable, but it is a health-research licence list and is explicitly incomplete on the English page.
- Landspitali and HH expose committee routes and approval responsibilities, not public misconduct decisions.
- IRF exposes strong misconduct consequences in the 2026 handbook, but not public case outcomes.

### Coding implications

- Upgrade the transparency layer from "No national body located" to a statutory-body-with-no-public-archive pattern.
- Treat the Committee on Good Scientific Practice as the strongest general research-integrity route.
- Treat National Bioethics Committee licences, Landspitali/HH committee pages, IMA clinical-trial routes, Data Protection Authority sources and IRF compliance clauses as boundary or project-governance lanes.
- Future work should locate the national committee's own website, procedure rules, annual reports and opinions if they are public; test the Icelandic version of the National Bioethics Committee licence list; and widen university committee-output checks beyond the current sample.
