# Estonia deep-dive sources

Updated: 23 April 2026

## Working system read

Estonia is best read as a hybrid transition system.

- The older backbone is still the code network: the 2002 Code of Ethics of Estonian Scientists and the 2017/2023 Estonian Code of Conduct for Research Integrity.
- The code layer remains institution-led. The 2017/2023 code explicitly leaves awareness, observance and procedural design to research institutions.
- Since 1 October 2025, the Organisation of Research and Development and Innovation Act (TAIKS) has added a statutory layer with research-ethics principles, institutional ethics-committee powers, a national Research Ethics Committee, and a separate committee for processing research ethics misconduct events.
- ETAG is the main public system hub because it combines integrity guidance, the new national committee route, grant ethics self-assessment material, ETIS workflow, and public signposting.

## Key conclusions supported by the source set

- Estonia should not be coded as one centralized misconduct-board country.
- The strongest national legal change is TAIKS, but it coexists with institution-led practice.
- The national Research Ethics Committee is an ethics-review route and publication node through ETIS.
- The misconduct-events committee is a statutory assessment and supervision-trigger route, not a clearly public sanction archive.
- ETAG is both the integrity-document hub and the main grant-compliance gate.
- Public visibility is better for ethics approvals, committee pages and decision lists than for misconduct findings.

## Gaps to revisit in a final sweep

- Public outputs, membership updates and first visible practice of the misconduct-events committee after launch.
- Stronger direct Human Genes Act and biobank route.
- More universities, hospitals and public institutes beyond the current sample.
- Clearer public trace for any national case-level publication beyond ethics approvals and TAI decision lists.

## Committee and case-file pass, 23 April 2026

### Seed bodies and routes checked

- ETAG Research Ethics Committee: national ethics-review route from January 2026, with ETIS submission and automatic publication of positive approvals.
- ETAG committee for processing research ethics misconduct events: national reporting, referral, second-opinion and possible supervision-trigger route.
- Institutional layer: University of Tartu research integrity counsellors and Research Ethics Committee, Tallinn University, TalTech, Estonian University of Life Sciences, TAI, Estonian Academy of Music and Theatre, and Estonian Literary Museum.
- Human genes, health data and biobank layer: EBIN, Ministry of Social Affairs transition pages, Human Genes Research Act, and the Estonian Biobank route.
- Boundary regimes: Ravimiamet and CTIS for clinical trials, AKI for research personal-data approvals, and PTA for animal-experiment authorization and non-technical summaries.
- HAKA remains relevant for quality assurance, but it is not a misconduct case-file route.

### Public case-material and publication findings

- ETAG's misconduct page is the clearest national handling source. It accepts written reports to ETAG, allows sufficiently evidenced anonymous reports, normally refers matters to the relevant institution, offers second opinions after institutional handling, can investigate if the institution does not proceed, and has no sanctioning power.
- The 2025 implementing regulation is the strongest publication-rule source. It points to generalized publication of commission proceedings/results and annual aggregate institutional statistics, not a live individual case archive.
- No public national archive of named, anonymized, or full misconduct decisions was found in this pass.
- University of Tartu publishes issued ethics approvals by year; the 2025 PDF is a representative approval-list file.
- TAI publishes annual Human Research Ethics Committee decision files for 2020-2025; the 2025 DOCX includes register numbers, project titles, responsible researchers or institutions, and TAIEK decision numbers and dates.
- EBIN annual reports and the Ministry of Social Affairs EBIN page provide boundary decision and application visibility for human genes and health-data access.
- Ravimiamet/CTIS, AKI research-data materials and PTA animal-project summary links are useful official transparency lanes, but they are boundary records rather than general research-misconduct files.

### Gaps and follow-up questions

- Monitor whether ETAG publishes the first generalized misconduct outcomes and annual institutional statistics after the new route has operated for a full cycle.
- Test how ETIS displays positive national Research Ethics Committee approvals after 2026 decisions start to accumulate.
- Widen institutional approval-list checks beyond UT and TAI, especially TalTech, Tallinn University, EMU, public institutes and hospitals.
- Check whether Estonian Academy of Music and Theatre and Estonian Literary Museum later publish approvals, minutes, activity summaries or only committee pages.
- Keep EBIN/biobank, CTIS, AKI and animal-project records separate from general misconduct publication when updating the transparency layer.
