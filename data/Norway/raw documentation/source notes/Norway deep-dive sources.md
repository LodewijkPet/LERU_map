# Norway deep-dive sources

Working note for the Norway committee-and-case-file pilot.

Updated: 23 April 2026.

## Starting point

Norway was already represented as a deep dossier in `data/countries.js`. The local folder only had `Overview Norway.docx` plus empty raw-documentation categories, while `data/source-registry.csv` had one transparency extraction entry for the GRU statements archive.

The pilot checked whether Norway is simply a strong GRU archive country, or whether several public case-material routes need to be coded in parallel.

## Committee and case-file pass

### Seed bodies

- Body: National Commission for the Investigation of Research Misconduct (GRU)
- Type: National misconduct appeal, guidance and reporting body
- Lane: Scientific misconduct and serious breaches of recognised research ethics norms
- Role: Guides institutions, receives institutional reports, acts as appeal body when an institution concludes scientific misconduct, can initiate cases, and publishes public statements.
- Strongest current source: GRU statements archive
- Publication owner: Norwegian National Research Ethics Committees / GRU
- Archive scope: Public GRU statements, especially appeal and own-initiative misconduct cases; not the full first-instance institutional case universe.
- Archive continuity: Structured archive visible from 2011 through 2026 in the current page, plus separate annual-report series.
- Referral path: Local institution first; institutional misconduct finding can be appealed to GRU; institutions report possible serious cases to GRU.
- Publication depth: Full public statements in HTML, with some older PDF statement files.
- Publicity mode: Proactive online publication for GRU statements; case documents may also become available through access rules after finalisation.
- Publication lifecycle: Case documents may be exempt while a case is under treatment; GRU public statement appears after the statement is final.
- Outcome maturity: Final national statement when in the GRU archive; institutional reports and active appeals are not necessarily public final case files.
- Publication class: Structured national statements archive plus annual-report synthesis.
- Accessibility status: Archive, latest 2026 statement and 2024 annual report were live in this pass.
- Case-file status: Strong national case-file route, but it mainly covers the GRU appeal/statement layer rather than every institutional first-instance case.
- Publication basis: Research Ethics Act, GRU role, FEK publication practice and public-administration access rules.
- Publication exclusions: First-instance institutional cases that do not produce a GRU statement are not systematically archived on the GRU statements page.
- Publication rule or retention: No fixed online retention period identified; final statements are publicly archived.

- Body: National Committee for Medical and Health Research Ethics (NEM)
- Type: National medical and health research ethics appeal body
- Lane: Appeals against REK/REK KULMU decisions and medical-health research ethics statements
- Role: Handles appeal decisions in the medical-health ethics approval subsystem and publishes a searchable decision database.
- Strongest current source: NEM appeal decisions database
- Publication owner: Norwegian National Research Ethics Committees / NEM
- Archive scope: NEM appeal decisions and statements, especially REK and REK KULMU appeal cases.
- Archive continuity: Structured database with 227 results in this pass and filters by decision outcome, first-instance body, institution and case type.
- Referral path: REK or REK KULMU first instance; appeal forwarded to NEM; NEM decision is final in the NEM lane.
- Publication depth: HTML case summaries and details, often with downloadable PDF decision letters.
- Publicity mode: Proactive online publication.
- Publication lifecycle: Final NEM decisions are published in the decision database.
- Outcome maturity: Final appeal decision in the medical-health ethics approval lane.
- Publication class: Structured boundary-regime decision database.
- Accessibility status: Live in this pass.
- Case-file status: Strong case-file source, but it is a medical-health ethics appeal route, not the general misconduct route.
- Publication basis: Health Research Act / Research Ethics Act appeal route and FEK publication practice.
- Publication exclusions: First-instance REK project decisions are not the same as NEM appeal decisions; REK has separate register and journal visibility.
- Publication rule or retention: No fixed retention period identified.

- Body: Regional Committees for Medical and Health Research Ethics (REK)
- Type: Regional medical and health research ethics pre-approval system
- Lane: Medical and health research projects, biobanks and confidentiality exemptions
- Role: Pre-approval and project administration; not a misconduct body.
- Strongest current source: REK portal applicant page and project register/postjournal references
- Publication owner: REK / REK portal
- Archive scope: Public project register and public postjournal, not a misconduct-case archive.
- Archive continuity: Current portal; postjournal only covers recent document traffic while the project register is the statutory public register.
- Referral path: REK first-instance decision; appeal can move to NEM.
- Publication depth: Project metadata, status and recent document journal information; decision documents require access handling.
- Publicity mode: Public register and postjournal; documents via access request.
- Publication lifecycle: Project information is registered as project/application data; postjournal has a recent-window function.
- Outcome maturity: Regulatory approval/project status, not misconduct outcome.
- Publication class: Public register / regulatory metadata / postjournal.
- Accessibility status: REK portal pages were live; the project registry is a separate application endpoint.
- Case-file status: Important boundary-regime source, but not a public research-integrity case-file archive.
- Publication basis: Health Research Act project-register duty and public-access rules.
- Publication exclusions: REK register metadata is not the same as NEM appeal decisions or GRU misconduct statements.
- Publication rule or retention: Public postjournal source describes recent journal visibility; register contents are public by statutory design.

- Body: Institutional research integrity committees
- Type: First-instance institutional misconduct routes
- Lane: Local handling of possible breaches of recognised research ethics norms
- Role: Receive reports, triage, investigate or issue statements, report serious possible breaches to GRU, and handle appeals before any GRU review.
- Strongest current sources: UiB processing page, NTNU Research Integrity Committee page and annual reports, OUS/UiO route, MF shared integrity committee guideline.
- Publication owner: Individual institution, with serious-case reporting to GRU.
- Archive scope: Mostly procedure pages, annual reports or public-document access after finalisation; no complete national institutional case archive found.
- Archive continuity: NTNU exposes annual reports from 2015 onward; other examples mainly expose procedure and routing.
- Referral path: Local department/faculty/institution first; serious cases to central committee and GRU reporting; misconduct findings can be appealed to GRU.
- Publication depth: Procedure, annual reports, and potential access to finalised documents; usually no proactive individual case archive.
- Publicity mode: Mostly procedure pages, annual reports and public-document access after the case is finalised.
- Publication lifecycle: Case material may be exempt while pending; access opens after finalisation except for confidential information.
- Outcome maturity: First-instance local handling, local statement, annual reporting or GRU appeal if a misconduct finding is made.
- Publication class: Procedure plus annual-report/public-document route.
- Accessibility status: UiB, NTNU, OUS/UiO and MF route pages were live in this pass.
- Case-file status: Strongly mapped route layer, but weak proactive local case-file publication.
- Publication basis: Research Ethics Act institutional duties, Public Administration Act and local guidelines.
- Publication exclusions: Many institutional first-instance cases never become public standalone case files unless appealed, reported in aggregate or requested after finalisation.
- Publication rule or retention: Public after finalisation is identified at UiB; systematic local retention/publication rules still need wider collection.

### Method notes

- Norway adds a "parallel public routes" rule. A country can have a strong national misconduct archive and still have another strong decision database in a boundary regime.
- Archive strength depends on route scope. GRU is strong for national misconduct statements, NEM is strong for medical-health ethics appeals, and REK is strong for regulatory register/postjournal visibility, but these are not interchangeable.
- Institutional documents should be coded separately from national statements. Norway has local-first handling, mandatory reporting of serious possible cases to GRU, and public access after finalisation, but not a complete proactive local case-file archive.
- Annual reports can expose the hidden pipeline. GRU's annual report shows institutional reports, inquiries and appeals that are not all visible as individual public statements.
- For future countries, add `route scope versus archive scope` even when the archive itself is excellent.
