## National legal and council layer

- Scientific Research and Innovation Activity Act (ZZrID): https://pisrs.si/api/datoteke/integracije/358724984
  Used for Articles 39-41, including the duties of research organizations, the national council, and the open-science frame.

  Used for the national strategy timeline and for the link between ethics, integrity, open science and research assessment.
- National Council for Ethics and Integrity in Science page: https://www.gov.si/zbirke/delovna-telesa/delovna-skupina/
  Main hub for mandate, membership, key documents and the public work-report link.

- Rules of operation of the National Council: https://www.gov.si/assets/ministrstva/MVZI/Znanost/Etika-in-integriteta-v-znanosti/Poslovnik-o-delu-Nacionalnega-sveta-za-etiko-in-integriteto-v-znanosti.pdf

- Procedure rules for adopting opinions: https://www.gov.si/assets/ministrstva/MVZI/Znanost/Etika-in-integriteta-v-znanosti/Pravila-postopka-za-sprejemanje-mnenj-o-skladnosti-ravnanja-z-eticnimi-pravili-in-o-krsitvah-nacel-raziskovalne-integritete.pdf

### Classification cautions

- Slovenian sources frequently combine ethics and integrity in the same body title. Do not assume every public output is a research-integrity case.
- National Council opinions, if issued, can be integrity opinions or ethical-rule opinions; classify each opinion by topic before aggregating.
- University of Ljubljana exposes a research-misconduct reporting route, while FOV exposes research-ethics approval identifiers. These are different publication classes.
- National Medical Ethics Committee and animal-procedure sources are boundary approval routes, not general misconduct publication.
- The 2023-2025 work report is important negative-publication evidence: it shows one ARIS-referred initiative and no proceeding, plus the lack of direct sanction power.

## Quality-consolidation pass - 30 April 2026

### Source currency

- The GOV.SI National Council page remains the current public hub. It lists the council as an independent body for ethical questions and conduct in scientific-research activity, says RKRS and KOsRIS established it on 18 October 2023 under ZZrID, gives an eleven-member composition, and still labels the current membership period as 2023-2026.
- The same GOV.SI hub now exposes the core current document set in one place: rules of operation, recommendations to research and higher-education organizations, procedure rules for adopting opinions and the 2023-2025 work report.
- The 3 July 2025 procedure remains the key transparency source. Article 8 requires an opinion to answer whether there is non-compliance with ethical rules or a justified allegation of breach of research-integrity principles; Article 9 requires the council to send opinions to parties, relevant institutional ethics committees and, for publicly funded projects, the funder or competent organization where relevant; it also requires publication of the opinion in anonymized form on the council website.
- No live page or archive of anonymized council opinions was located in this retest. Keep the transparency class at "publication rule but no live opinion archive" until an actual opinion file appears.
- The ReZrIS30 2022-2023 implementation report is a useful source-current check: it records ethics/integrity actions as partly future-facing, including national guidelines, promotion/education, funder criteria for ethics/integrity or sanctions, and identifying sensitive areas needing national or institutional ethics-review bodies. It also shows that as of 31 December 2023 the national-guideline indicator could not yet be measured.
- ARIS remains a funder and implementation actor rather than a case publisher. Its mission page says it follows open-science and scientific-integrity policies, the 2025 annual report confirms stable financing and proto-evaluation machinery, and the stable-financing act page confirms the current 18 January 2025 general-act baseline. No ARIS misconduct decision archive was located in this pass.

### ENRIO status

- ENRIO has a Slovenia country report. The report still opens with older "does not yet have a national institutional structure" wording, but it also records the 18 October 2023 establishment of the National Council under the Scientific Research and Innovation Activity Act. Code it as a secondary comparison source that needs official-source reconciliation, not as the controlling current description.
- ENRIO's Slovenia member page confirms the National Council for Ethics and Integrity in Science as an ENRIO member since 2024 and describes it as an independent, legally based body.
- ENRIO's 2024 annual report records the National Council as a new 2024 ENRIO member and separately notes the Commission for Equal Opportunities in Science leaving the member list. This helps explain the transition from the older CEOS/CWS route to the current council route.

### Main code baseline

- National legal baseline: ZZrID Article 39 and the council recommendations remain the strongest national baseline. The recommendations explicitly connect research integrity with reliable and responsible research and honest reporting, call for institutional codes or guidelines, and point to the European Code of Conduct for Research Integrity as the framework-programme baseline.
- National council baseline: the council applies the European Code of Conduct for Research Integrity in procedure, but the council's recommendations do not yet amount to a fully separate Slovenian national research-integrity code with a public case archive.
- Institutional baseline: UL, UM, UP and ZRC SAZU remain the main public route examples. UM explicitly incorporates the ALLEA code in its code architecture; UL and ZRC SAZU provide the clearest research-misconduct and institute breach-handling route examples.
- European baseline: ALLEA 2023 should remain coded as the European/Horizon Europe reference baseline. It is not a substitute for checking Slovenian institutional rules, because the Slovenian council still asks organizations to build their own codes, procedures and committees.

### Coding conclusion

The quality pass strengthens the existing Slovenia coding without changing the transparency tier. Slovenia now has a clearer ENRIO-aligned national council than the older ENRIO country-report opening suggests, but the official sources still show an advisory/opinion and harmonization model rather than a sanctions tribunal. The strongest new quality refinements are: ENRIO member status since 2024, official reconciliation of the council route against the still-partly-outdated ENRIO report wording, a clearer ALLEA/European-code baseline, ARIS as a funder/implementation route rather than a misconduct publisher, and an explicit watchpoint for future anonymized council opinions.
