# Luxembourg deep-dive sources

Updated: 24 April 2026

## Committee-and-case-file pass

Luxembourg remains a compact LARI-centred research-integrity system. The Luxembourg Agency for Research Integrity (LARI) is the national research integrity office for its member organisations and FNR-funded research, and the Commission for Research Integrity (CRI) is the independent investigation route. The current evidence supports a summary-based transparency code rather than an archive code: LARI publishes governance documents and aggregate annual reports, but CRI final reports are confidential and no public decision repository was located.

The LARI about page still marks the Annual Report 2023/2024 as "coming soon" in this pass. The latest public LARI practice report remains the 2020/2021/2022 annual report, which records 7 cases opened, 6 closed, 1 continued and 90 requests during the interim period. This should be indexed as aggregate practice evidence, not as case-file publication.

The strongest public case-level Luxembourg signal is the FNR statement of 29 April 2020 concerning a CRI investigation into an FNR-funded project. It reports that the CRI found no evidence of fabrication, falsification or plagiarism, but identified questionable practices in communication of results, conflicts of interest and possible confidentiality breach, with recommendations to the host institution, principal investigator and FNR. This is a rare funder communication in a confidential system, not a recurring archive.

CNER is more public than the misconduct route, but it is a health-research ethics boundary. Its publications page is current to 31 March 2026 and links activity reports through 2024 plus a list of studies with favourable opinions. The favourable-opinion list found in this pass runs from 2000 to October 2025. These are project-level ethics approval materials, not research-misconduct decisions.

The animal-use boundary also has public reporting. The University Animal Experimentation Ethics Committee is the institutional opinion route, the LCSB page explains that projects then require Ministry of Agriculture and Ministry of Health authorisation, and ALVA publishes annual animal-use statistics. The 2024 ALVA animal-use report should be treated as aggregate authorisation and welfare reporting, not a misconduct file.

## Coding conclusion

Keep Luxembourg at score 2: summary-based national reporting with a rare case-level funder communication. Do not upgrade it to a fragmented archive unless LARI, FNR or a member organisation exposes a recurring list of individual CRI reports, decision summaries or case files. Keep CNER and ALVA as stronger boundary-publication lanes, not as substitutes for misconduct transparency.
