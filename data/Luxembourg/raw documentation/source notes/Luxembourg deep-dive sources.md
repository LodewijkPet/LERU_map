# Luxembourg deep-dive sources

Updated: 29 April 2026

## Committee-and-case-file pass

Luxembourg remains a compact LARI-centred research-integrity system. The Luxembourg Agency for Research Integrity (LARI) is the national research integrity office for its member organisations and FNR-funded research, and the Commission for Research Integrity (CRI) is the independent investigation route. The current evidence supports a summary-based transparency code rather than an archive code: LARI publishes governance documents and aggregate annual reports, but CRI final reports are confidential and no public decision repository was located.

The LARI about page still marks the Annual Report 2023/2024 as "coming soon" in this pass. The latest public LARI practice report remains the 2020/2021/2022 annual report, which records 7 cases opened, 6 closed, 1 continued and 90 requests during the interim period. This should be indexed as aggregate practice evidence, not as case-file publication.

The strongest public case-level Luxembourg signal is the FNR statement of 29 April 2020 concerning a CRI investigation into an FNR-funded project. It reports that the CRI found no evidence of fabrication, falsification or plagiarism, but identified questionable practices in communication of results, conflicts of interest and possible confidentiality breach, with recommendations to the host institution, principal investigator and FNR. This is a rare funder communication in a confidential system, not a recurring archive.

CNER is more public than the misconduct route, but it is a health-research ethics boundary. Its publications page is current to 31 March 2026 and links activity reports through 2024 plus a list of studies with favourable opinions. The favourable-opinion list found in this pass runs from 2000 to October 2025. These are project-level ethics approval materials, not research-misconduct decisions.

The animal-use boundary also has public reporting. The University Animal Experimentation Ethics Committee is the institutional opinion route, the LCSB page explains that projects then require Ministry of Agriculture and Ministry of Health authorisation, and ALVA publishes annual animal-use statistics. The 2024 ALVA animal-use report should be treated as aggregate authorisation and welfare reporting, not a misconduct file.

## Coding conclusion

Keep Luxembourg at score 2: summary-based national reporting with a rare case-level funder communication. Do not upgrade it to a fragmented archive unless LARI, FNR or a member organisation exposes a recurring list of individual CRI reports, decision summaries or case files. Keep CNER and ALVA as stronger boundary-publication lanes, not as substitutes for misconduct transparency.

## Quality-consolidation pass - 29 April 2026

### Source currency

- The LARI about page remains the live national source for governance, CRI membership, Board composition, statute, CRI rules, data-sharing agreement, FNR MoU and annual-report links. It still marks the Annual Report 2023/2024 as "coming soon"; the latest public LARI practice report remains the 2020/2021/2022 report.
- LARI's current investigation page confirms the CRI route, anonymous reporting possibility, 30-business-day rejection notice for non-credible or out-of-remit allegations, confidential final reports, five-month target for investigations and member-organisation responsibility for implementing recommendations.
- The 2024 CRI Rules of Procedure, the 2025 LARI-member data-sharing agreement and the 2025 LARI-FNR MoU remain the current procedure backbone located in this pass.
- FNR's current funding-policies page still requires grantees to follow the European Code of Conduct, FNR research-integrity guidelines, ethics-clearance rules, clinical-trial registration, animal-research requirements, open-access and research-data expectations, and to declare well-founded suspicion of misconduct to LARI with notification to FNR.
- The University of Luxembourg research-integrity and ERP pages remain current 2026 route evidence: suspected misconduct is triaged inside the University and credible allegations transfer to LARI, while the ERP expressly excludes suspected research misconduct and publishes 2026 meeting/application deadlines for ethics review.
- CNER's publications page was last updated on 31 March 2026 and still exposes activity reports through 2024 plus the favourable-opinion list. ALVA's 2024 animal-use publication page was last updated on 9 December 2025 and remains an animal-authorisation reporting lane, not misconduct transparency.

### ENRIO status

- ENRIO has a Luxembourg country report, last updated June 2024. It confirms the current app reading: Luxembourg has a national research-integrity office in LARI, LARI/CRI has the exclusive mandate for alleged research misconduct at LARI member organisations and FNR-funded research, final reports go to the relevant member organisation(s), and annual reports are the normal public transparency channel.
- ENRIO's 2024 annual report lists the Luxembourg Agency for Research Integrity as Luxembourg's ENRIO member and says ENRIO country reports were reviewed and updated in 2024. Use this as secondary comparison evidence, not as a replacement for LARI, FNR, University, LIST, LIH, LISER, CNER or ALVA primary sources.

### Main code baseline

- Main national soft-law baseline: LARI uses the European Code of Conduct for Research Integrity as the key normative reference and describes its investigation scope by reference to the latest version of the European Code.
- Main funder baseline: FNR funding policies require compliance with the European Code, FNR Research Integrity guidelines and ethics/data/animal/open-science obligations, with LARI notification for well-founded misconduct suspicion.
- University baseline: the University of Luxembourg follows the European Code and the European Charter and Code for Researchers, mandates Good Scientific Practice training for doctoral candidates and transfers credible misconduct allegations to LARI.
- LIST baseline: the public LIST Code of Ethics, effective 28 September 2021, is the reference for LIST Ethics Committee assessments. It endorses the European Code and covers research integrity, social responsibility, human participants, animals, data, dissemination, dual use, risk and whistleblowing. The code states that individual employee misconduct is not the responsibility of the LIST Ethics Committee, except for advisory support to managers.
- LIH baseline: the public QSMO page places Research Quality and Ethics inside LIH's integrated QMS and lists the European Code of Conduct among the guiding norms. LIH doctoral material says a procedure for suspected scientific misconduct exists and the plagiarism guideline states LIH has an alleged-scientific-misconduct procedure, but no standalone public procedure or case repository was located.

### Coding conclusion

The 29 April 2026 quality pass confirms the committee-and-case-file coding rather than changing it. Luxembourg is an ENRIO-aligned, LARI-centred national system with confidential CRI final reports and summary-based public transparency. The annual-report series is now seeded as an extraction corridor: 2018 gives the first ongoing case signal, 2019 gives the richest anonymized row-level table, and 2020/2021/2022 gives the latest aggregate case/request counts and recommendation categories. The 2020 FNR statement remains a rare case-level funder communication, not evidence of a recurring case archive. LIST and LIH add useful institutional code and quality routes, but neither currently exposes a public misconduct-output repository in this pass.
