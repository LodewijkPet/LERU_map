# Spain deep-dive sources

Updated: 24 April 2026

## System reading

Spain is best read as a polycentric research-integrity system with a consultative national core, strong institutional execution, and a genuinely relevant subnational layer.

- CEEI is the national reference and consultative body, but not a single national misconduct tribunal.
- The national legal core sits in Ley 14/2011 and the CEEI regulation in RD 53/2023, as amended by RD 1045/2025.
- CEEI's own public documents make the system legible through reports, recommendations, admission instructions and composition material.
- General execution remains institutional through public research organisations, universities, local committees, codes, ombuds routes and disciplinary channels.
- CSIC and ISCIII are not just examples; they are major integrity ecosystems inside the broader Spanish system.
- Catalonia matters analytically because CIR-CAT and the CERCA Ombudsperson create a real subnational layer.
- Biomedical, clinical and animal-research governance is highly visible and must stay separate from general integrity handling.

## Key conclusions for coding

1. Spain should be coded as a multi-level consultative and institutionally implemented system, not as a centralized national misconduct-board model.
2. CEEI is the national reference point and can issue reports, recommendations and pronouncements, but ordinary execution still sits mainly with institutions and sector bodies.
3. The 2015 declaration and the current CEEI procedure recommendations are core national soft-law anchors even though Spain does not revolve around one dominant national code.
4. CSIC and ISCIII deserve explicit visibility because they host central internal integrity architectures of their own.
5. Catalonia is not just local colour. CIR-CAT and CERCA show a real subnational integrity route that should stay visible in the Spanish system map.
6. Spain's public transparency is distributed and partly regionalized rather than archive-based.

## Public-transparency reading

Spain is not a national archive system and no national searchable archive of individual misconduct findings was identified in this pass.

Public visibility is strongest for:

- laws and regulations
- CEEI reports, recommendations and administrative documents
- CIR-CAT procedure and recommendation pages
- institutional committee portals and codes
- specialist regulator and ethics-boundary pages

The most accurate transparency reading is therefore:

- stronger than a pure no-publication model because Spain exposes substantive national and regional integrity outputs
- weaker than a true archive model because publication remains distributed and does not amount to a unified searchable case archive

## Committee and case-file pass

Date: 24 April 2026

### Seed bodies

- Body: CEEI
- Type: National consultative reference body
- Lane: Research integrity, responsible research and ethics in scientific and technical research
- Role: Issues reports, proposals, recommendations, pronouncements, agreements and second opinions; can request information for its consultative work after RD 1045/2025.
- Strongest current source: CEEI official page and admission instructions.
- Publication owner: Ministry/CEEI page.
- Archive scope: Reports, recommendations and administrative documents; no complete individual decision archive located.
- Database function: Route and output hub, not a misconduct decision database.
- Micro-publication node: Salamanca-related report trace on the CEEI page.
- Individual case visibility: Isolated report trace plus issue-based recommendations; not a searchable case archive.
- Public data elements: Document title, type, date or topic, committee route and downloadable PDF.
- Non-public or restricted elements: Complete file, evidence record, statements, deliberation material and full case file held under confidentiality.
- Why this is or is not a misconduct case file: CEEI can work on conflicts and publish outputs, but the public page is a consultative-output hub rather than an archive of findings or sanctions.
- Publication basis: Admission instructions state that publication of opinions, conclusions and activity reports is on the department website and subject to RD 53/2023, transparency rules and ministry web-management rules.

- Body: CIR-CAT
- Type: Catalan regional research-integrity committee
- Lane: Subnational research integrity and good scientific practice
- Role: Advises Catalan research-performing and research-funding actors, analyzes and prevents research-integrity conflicts, and handles communications and requests.
- Strongest current source: CIR-CAT official page, admission and processing procedure, and Recommendation 1/2025.
- Publication owner: Generalitat de Catalunya.
- Archive scope: Public procedure plus at least one substantive recommendation; no complete opinion archive identified in this pass.
- Database function: Regional route page and isolated recommendation PDF, not a database.
- Micro-publication node: Recommendation 1/2025 on UB/CERCA affiliation in scientific publications.
- Individual case visibility: Concrete institutional dispute context and recommendation, but not a full case-file archive.
- Public data elements: Parties/institutions, dates, background, reasoning, conclusions and recommended action.
- Non-public or restricted elements: Complete case file, full evidentiary record and deliberation material remain with the secretariat.
- Why this is or is not a misconduct case file: Recommendation 1/2025 is case-like integrity output because it responds to a concrete request and assesses a publication-affiliation practice, but it is a recommendation rather than a sanction or national decision.
- Publication basis: Procedure states that publication of CIR-CAT opinions follows Generalitat transparency rules.

- Body: CEIm/AEMPS, biomedical ethics, animal-project and other specialist ethics routes
- Type: Boundary regimes
- Lane: Clinical trials, biomedical research, animal research, human samples/data and specialised legal review
- Role: Review, approval, registration, authorisation or advisory functions.
- Strongest current source: AEMPS CEIm page, Ley 14/2007, RD 1090/2015, MICIU animal project evaluation page and RD 53/2013.
- Publication owner: Specialist regulator or competent authority.
- Archive scope: Approval/register/route visibility, not general research-misconduct publication.
- Why this is or is not a misconduct case file: These records can signal compliance issues but are ethics or regulatory approvals and should not be merged with integrity case publication.
