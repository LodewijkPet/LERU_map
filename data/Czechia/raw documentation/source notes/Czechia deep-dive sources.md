# Czechia deep-dive sources

Accessed: 24 April 2026; quality check updated 30 April 2026

## Quality-consolidation pass

### Source currency and ENRIO cross-check

- ENRIO country report: https://www.enrio.eu/country-reports/czech-republic/
- Status: ENRIO's May 2024 Czech Republic country report confirms the core app coding: Czechia has no national research-integrity commission with competence across all university and non-university research institutions. ENRIO treats the Commission for the Scientific Integrity of the CAS as the main named route, but restricted to CAS and its institutes.
- ENRIO membership: https://www.enrio.eu/members/czech-republic/ and the ENRIO Annual Report 2024 list the Commission for Scientific Integrity of the Czech Academy of Sciences as the Czech ENRIO member; the member page records ENRIO membership since 2015.
- CAS currency: the current CAS committee page still gives composition and secretariat only. The CAS 2024 annual report is useful for source currency because it records the 2024 code-amendment process and the 24-26 September 2024 "Scientific Integrity and the Role of Science" conference, but it does not create a public case-output archive.

### Main code baseline

- National baseline remains the 2005 Ethical Framework of Research and Resolution No. 1005/2005.
- European baseline: the ALLEA European Code of Conduct for Research Integrity, revised edition 2023, should be kept as the Horizon/European reference. The current CAS Code of Ethics for Researchers came into effect on 16 April 2024 and uses the national research ethics framework, European and international instruments, and local codes as reference material.
- CAS baseline: the 2024 CAS researcher code is the strongest current Czech academy-level code, covering fabrication/falsification/plagiarism, authorship, data preservation, conflicts of interest, review duties, reporting of breaches, pressure, foreign influence and institute-level supplementation.
- Funder baseline: GA CR's 2027 tender documents keep the explicit institutional-ethics-code and transparent-breach-process requirement. TA CR adds a provider-side internal ethics code for employees, evaluators and advisory bodies; this is governance and conflict-of-interest evidence, not a research-misconduct case archive.

### Public-output retest

- Charles University remains the strongest Czech public institutional micro-publication archive located in this work: statements from 2014 through 2025, minutes through 2025, privacy-based non-publication notes and representative reasoned statements.
- University of South Bohemia / JCU adds a second public institutional output corridor that was missed in the 24 April pass. Its Czech Ethics Commission page exposes committee composition, rules, statements and minutes from 2015 through 20 August 2025. It includes at least one academic-integrity/thesis-plagiarism statement from 12 April 2023 and recent minutes on workplace communication or academic-speech matters. This widens the Czech transparency picture, but it remains an institutional mixed-ethics archive, not a national research-misconduct archive.
- JCU's research-ethics-and-integrity page also states that the university-wide Ethics Committee handles Code of Ethics compliance including principles of science and research, while faculty ethics committees handle project or study opinions for human participants, biological material or identifiable data. The page currently contains an unrelated spam-like inserted paragraph; rely on the committee and document links, and treat the page as a source needing later manual hygiene checking.
- Masaryk University adds research ethics opinions and recommendations on its Research Ethics Committee documents page, but these are project/research-ethics guidance outputs rather than misconduct decisions.

### Route-directory effect

- Country coding stays distributed: national framework plus CAS, public universities, funders and boundary regulators.
- Transparency should now be phrased as "institutional statement archives" rather than a single Charles University archive. Charles is still the strongest and deepest current archive; JCU is a smaller but real second micro-publication node.
- CAS remains route-visible and ENRIO-visible, with confidential sessions and no located public decision/opinion archive in this pass.
- Funders remain leverage routes: GA CR has strong host-institution and funding-consequence language; TA CR has internal ethics and anti-corruption/conflict-of-interest governance; AZV requires health-research ethics and animal documentation.
- Boundaries remain separate: research ethics approval, clinical trials, animal research, data protection, open science and research security should not be upgraded into general misconduct transparency.

### Added extraction targets

- Charles University Ethics Commission statements and minutes: index by year, topic, route, privacy exclusion and outcome.
- JCU Ethics Commission statements and minutes: index by year, document type, matter type, public/restricted elements and whether the item concerns research integrity, thesis/student integrity, workplace conduct, academic speech or project ethics.
- CAS annual reports: use as annual-report/currency evidence and search for Scientific Integrity Committee mentions, but do not treat the reports as case-output archives unless case counts or outcomes are found.
- GA CR and TA CR: extract tender/code wording by provider route, consequence language, reviewer/evaluator scope and publication status.

### Negative findings and cautions

- No single Czech national research-misconduct case archive was identified.
- No public CAS Scientific Integrity Committee case-output archive was identified from the current CAS committee page.
- Masaryk University is a useful official route example, but no misconduct decision archive was located in this pass. University of South Bohemia / JCU should no longer be treated as output-light: the 30 April 2026 quality check located its Czech Ethics Commission statements and minutes corridor through 20 August 2025.
- The Institute of Ethnology CAS page shows a CAS institute-level ethics committee and code route, which is useful for fragmentation, but not a case-output archive.
- GA CR EXPRO 2027 is strong consequence evidence, not a public funder case-file source.
- SUKL, clinical-trial ethics committees, faculty research ethics committees, animal-research bodies, GDPR and research-security routes should remain boundary lanes unless a source directly joins them to misconduct handling.
