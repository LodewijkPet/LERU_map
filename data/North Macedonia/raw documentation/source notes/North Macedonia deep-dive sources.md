## Committee and case-file pass

### Seed bodies

- Body: Ethics Board under the Law on Scientific-Research Activity
- Type: National statutory ethics body
- Lane: Scientific-research ethics and values
- Role: Monitor and evaluate ethical principles and values in scientific-research activity, human integrity in research and ethical business relations among research actors.
- Strongest current source: Ministry-hosted Law on Scientific-Research Activity, Article 14.
- Publication owner: Government/Ministry route, but no current public Ethics Board page, recommendation page or annual-report page was located in this pass.
- Archive scope: Procedure-only in located public sources.
- Case-file status: No public national misconduct case archive located.
- Why this is or is not a misconduct case file: The law creates a national ethics body and says its opinions/positions are recommendations and that it reports annually to Government, but the located public layer does not expose recommendation files, case decisions or anonymized summaries.

- Body: National Council for Higher Education and Scientific Research Activity
- Type: National quality, standards and member-conduct body
- Lane: Higher-education/scientific-research governance and member ethics
- Role: Publishes standards, programmes, reports, ethical code, ethical-board decision and risk material.
- Strongest current source: National Council homepage/document portal.
- Publication owner: National Council.
- Archive scope: Public document portal.
- Case-file status: Governance/report corridor; not a general misconduct archive.
- Publication class: Procedure and annual/report publication for council work; member-conduct ethical-board route only.
- Why this is or is not a misconduct case file: The Ethical Code creates a three-member Ethical Board for National Council member conduct, complaint handling, warnings, reports and notice to the council, but it does not create a public repository of general researcher misconduct cases.

- Body: Agency for Quality in Higher Education (AQHE), Evaluation Board and Accreditation Board
- Type: Quality-assurance agency and board network
- Lane: External evaluation, self-evaluation, accreditation and doctoral-mentor accreditation
- Role: Publishes evaluation reports and monthly accreditation/mentor decision lists.
- Strongest current source: AQHE Evaluation Board report page and Accreditation Board decision page.
- Publication owner: AQHE.
- Archive scope: Quality-assurance report and decision corridors.
- Case-file status: Strong public QA repository; not a misconduct repository.
- Why this is or is not a misconduct case file: The files can expose ethics, plagiarism, complaint and quality infrastructure, but their decision function is accreditation/evaluation rather than adjudicating research misconduct.

- Body: UKIM central Ethics Commission and unit-level ethics commissions
- Type: Institutional ethics route
- Lane: University code, teachers/associates, students and professional/administrative service
- Role: UKIM Ethical Code creates the University Ethics Commission and unit-level commissions, with report rejection, warning, public warning and referral routes.
- Strongest current source: UKIM Ethical Code page and PDF.
- Publication owner: UKIM.
- Archive scope: Procedure/contact page found; no central public decision archive located.
- Case-file status: Procedure-only centrally; institutional self-evaluation reports provide micro-publication snippets.
- Public data elements: Committee email, code route, disciplinary/plagiarism summaries in some unit self-evaluation reports.
- Non-public or restricted elements: Individual ethics-commission files and central case decisions were not located as a public archive.

## Quality-consolidation pass - 29 April 2026

### Source currency

- The Ministry-hosted Law on Scientific-Research Activity remains the current source for the statutory Ethics Board. Article 14 confirms the nine-member board, its code/rules role, recommendation format and annual-report duty to Government. No current public Ethics Board appointment page, recommendation archive, annual report or case-decision page was located in this pass.
- The Ministry page for National Council documents and the council's own document portal remain current entry points for council rules, member lists, the member Ethical Code, the Ethical Board decision, reports, risk plans and programme material. The council portal now exposes a four-year report for June 2021-June 2025 as a high-priority indexing target.
- AQHE remains the strongest public repository corridor, but the function is quality assurance. The Evaluation Board reports page exposes five current report PDFs, and the Accreditation Board decision hub exposes monthly study-programme and doctoral-mentor decisions through March 2026 on page 1, with older decisions continuing on page 2.
- UKIM now has a central self-evaluation directory for unit reports and a current 2023/2024 university self-evaluation report. The directory lists faculties, institutes, public scientific institutions and the Orthodox theological faculty; use it as the main UKIM extraction target rather than relying only on the Medicine and TMF examples.
- UKIM's evaluation page confirms that self-evaluations are continuous and that UKIM has passed five EUA external-evaluation processes. That is QA evidence, not misconduct adjudication, but it explains why self-evaluation files are a durable evidence corridor.
- A Law Faculty UKIM self-evaluation report was found as a new unit seed. A quick text search did not locate obvious plagiarism or discipline wording in that PDF, so it should be indexed as a QA/institutional-governance source before any integrity conclusion is drawn from it.
- SEEU's current Senate page confirms additional institutional route evidence: the Senate appoints the Student Ombudsperson, selects an authorized person for corruption allegations, approves the student Code of Ethics, establishes the Professional Council for Doctorate Studies and approves the Rector's annual report. No public SEEU case-output archive was found.

### ENRIO status

- ENRIO's updated country-report archive does not list North Macedonia. This is comparison evidence only; it supports the conclusion that North Macedonia is outside ENRIO's country-report set, but it does not prove absence of domestic statutory, QA or institutional routes.
- ENRIO's 2024 annual report does not list a North Macedonian member organisation among 2024 ENRIO members or affiliates. Use this as network evidence, not as a primary source on national law or institutional practice.

### Main code baseline

- National statutory baseline: the Scientific-Research Activity Law says the Ethics Board adopts an ethics code for scientific-research work, publication of results, relations among researchers, competition-related procedures and relations with the public/media. A public copy of that board code was not located in this pass.
- National Council baseline: the council's Ethical Code applies to National Council members and creates a member-conduct Ethical Board route. It should not be treated as a national researcher misconduct code.
- UKIM baseline: the 10 May 2021 UKIM Ethical Code is the strongest public institutional code found. It creates central and unit-level ethics commissions and warning/public-warning routes; the UKIM open-science policy and AI declaration add current data, repository, copyright, personal-data and generative-AI boundaries.
- SEEU baseline: the statute and third-cycle rule require Code of Ethics compliance, and the Senate page confirms the Senate approves the student Code of Ethics. The current public Code of Ethics text and any ombudsperson/corruption-output practice still need extraction.
- European baseline: the ALLEA European Code of Conduct for Research Integrity, revised 2023, remains the European and Horizon Europe reference framework. No direct national adoption or translation by a North Macedonian body was located in this pass.
