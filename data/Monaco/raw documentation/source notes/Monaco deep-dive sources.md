# Monaco deep-dive sources

Access date: 28 April 2026

## Initial app profile and committee/case-file seed pass

Monaco has no local overview document in this folder. This pass builds a first
app dossier from official public sources and should be treated as a seed profile,
not a completed committee-and-case-file pilot.

## System reading

The current evidence supports a microstate, institution-led model:

- The Centre Scientifique de Monaco (CSM) is the main public research
  institution. Legimonaco and CSM sources show a public-establishment mandate
  for scientific and medical observation, basic research, applied research,
  project assessment and partial financing.
- CSM has a board and a Scientific and Technical Committee. These are research
  governance and scientific assessment actors, not located research-misconduct
  case handlers.
- International University of Monaco (IUM) is the main higher-education
  institution with government-recognized diplomas. IUM publishes a governance
  page, accreditation page and ethics policy/code of conduct that covers
  intellectual honesty and integrity in learning, teaching, research and
  community service.
- Biomedical research is governed by Law No. 1.265 of 23 December 2002 and the
  Comite consultatif d'ethique en matiere de recherche biomedicale. This is a
  health-research approval and protection-of-persons route, not a general
  misconduct archive.
- Innovation funding, Horizon Europe participation, APDP data protection,
  MCIPO intellectual property, and animal/veterinary rules are boundary regimes.

No national general research-misconduct body, national research-misconduct
decision archive, CSM misconduct output page, IUM misconduct case archive, or
biomedical-ethics decision archive was located in this pass.

## Seed handling routes

### CSM public research governance route

The CSM law and governance page identify the public research institution, board
and Scientific and Technical Committee. The institution publishes scientific
outputs and documentation, but no research-misconduct procedure, ombudsperson,
case archive or annual misconduct statistics were located.

### IUM higher-education and academic-integrity route

IUM is licensed to deliver diplomas and its diplomas are recognized by the
Government of Monaco through Ministerial Act No. 2023-575 of 27 September 2023.
Its ethics policy is binding on the university community and includes
intellectual honesty and integrity in learning, teaching, research and community
service. The public pages do not expose a research-misconduct complaint route,
disciplinary decisions or case statistics.

### Biomedical research ethics route

Law No. 1.265 requires human biomedical research projects to be submitted to the
Comite consultatif d'ethique en matiere de recherche biomedicale. It also gives
the Minister of State suspension/interdiction powers and creates criminal
penalties for some unlawful research conduct. This is a strong protection-of-
persons and clinical-research route, not a general research-integrity archive.

### Clinical research network route

Government press releases document CSM-Princess Grace Hospital clinical research
activity and the 7 February 2025 CSM-INSERM agreement creating the Monaco
Clinical Research Infrastructure Network (M-CRIN). This is important for the
biomedical research map, but no case-publication route was found.

### Innovation, data, IP and animal boundaries

The Monaco Innovation Fund and Horizon Europe pages show R&D funding access and
co-funding routes for enterprises and entities. APDP/CCIN sources provide
data-protection reporting and deliberation corridors. MCIPO provides IP register
visibility. Animal treatment and veterinary laws provide animal-welfare and
veterinary oversight, but no direct animal-experiment permit register was located.

## Public case-material finding

Positive but limited public material:

- IUM ethics policy and code of conduct: institutional conduct norm, not a case
  archive.
- Biomedical research law: committee review, ministerial suspension/interdiction

- APDP/CCIN reports and data-protection law: boundary transparency for data and

- MCIPO databases: IP register visibility, not misconduct publication.
