# Research Integrity Routes And Transparency At LERU Member Universities

Status date: 2026-06-23

## Purpose

This is a public-source pilot summary for member validation. It is based on official public sources and existing project data. It is not an official LERU statement, not an institutional audit and not a substitute for member confirmation.

## Executive Summary

This public-source pilot represents all 24 official LERU member universities and maps the visible research-integrity routes, offices, committees, procedure documents and public-output signals identified in this pass. All 24 profiles are now detailed public-source seeds, and all 24 have identifiable public institutional procedure or route evidence. The report is therefore useful as a validation product: it gives LERU / INTE members a structured route map to confirm, correct or add to, while staying outside private member communications and unpublished case material.

The central finding is that procedure visibility is more consistent than public-output visibility. Strict public-output evidence is currently recorded for 14 member profiles. The public-output typology gives a more precise interpretation: 13 profiles show institution-owned local output, 4 rely on national, regional or sector-level output, 5 are procedure-visible but output-light, 1 has restricted or internal output signals, and 1 is historical or case-specific rather than a current standing output channel. No profile is currently coded as boundary-only or unclear.

These categories describe public-source visibility, not case incidence. Absence of public output should not be interpreted as absence of cases, concerns, investigations or internal reporting. Annual statements, aggregate statistics, anonymized summaries, public case PDFs, national statements, restricted committee material and historical case-complex pages should be read as different transparency signals. Boundary regimes also need careful separation: research ethics review, clinical governance, animal research, data protection, quality assurance, student discipline, IP/open science and research security are included only where useful for context and should not be collapsed into research-misconduct handling unless the public source explicitly connects them.

## Key Findings

- LERU institutions are generally procedure-visible: each member profile now has identifiable public institutional route or procedure evidence.
- Public-output visibility is more uneven than procedure visibility.
- National, regional and sector-level reporting can obscure institution-level comparability.
- Annual statements, aggregate reports and anonymized summaries are more common than full case-file archives.
- Boundary regimes are often visible but should not be confused with research-misconduct handling.
- Member validation is needed to confirm internal routes, current office names, non-public reporting practices and any public sources missed in this pass.

## Validation Agenda

- Is this the correct public institutional route for research-integrity concerns?
- Is the named office, committee, ombudsperson, adviser network or referent current?
- Are annual reports, aggregate statistics, anonymized summaries or case-learning outputs public, internal or absent?
- Are national, regional or sector reports interpreted correctly in relation to the institution?
- Which operational details should remain unpublished in a public validation product?
- Which public sources should be added, corrected, replaced or retired?

## Limitations

- This is a public-source draft based on official pages, public documents and existing project data.
- Search, language and source-currency limitations remain.
- The summary does not claim exhaustiveness and should not be interpreted as an institutional audit.
- Absence of public output should not be interpreted as absence of cases, concerns, investigations or internal reporting.
- Institutional and national levels may overlap; national or sector output is not automatically institution-owned output.
- Research ethics, data protection, clinical ethics, animal research, student discipline, quality assurance, IP/open science and research security are boundary regimes unless explicitly connected to research-misconduct handling.

## Suggested Next Discussion With LERU / INTE Members

The most useful next step is a validation conversation around the public route map rather than a new source-extraction round. Members can check whether the displayed route is current, whether public or internal annual reporting exists, whether national or sector outputs are interpreted correctly, and which public sources should be added or corrected before the report is used more widely.
