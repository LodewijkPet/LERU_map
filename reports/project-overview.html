LERU map pilot

Research Integrity Systems in Europe

A static, source-linked web app and evidence corpus for mapping how European research integrity systems are organized, documented, and made publicly visible.

Open the map app Open the protocol Read extraction status

Summary

This project turns scattered national policies, research integrity procedures, public body pages, annual reports, and case-publication routes into a navigable European map. The current pilot combines a browser-based interface with structured country data, local source files, extraction logs, and a source registry.

Core idea. The map is not only a visual country list. It is a working evidence system: each country profile records governance actors, document categories, extraction signals, next corpus steps, and public transparency information where available.

What It Is

The project has two connected parts: a public-facing static app and a research corpus behind it.

The Web App

The main page, index.html, displays a Europe map, a searchable country list, country detail panels, and full dossier sections. Users can select a country on the map or in the list, inspect the system profile, open collected files, and follow the recorded next research step.

The Evidence Corpus

The data folder contains country overview documents, raw documentation, map data, source logs, app datasets, and the transparency layer. The corpus is designed so the interface can stay simple while the evidence trail remains visible and extendable.

The Protocol

The separate protocol page describes the planned mapping logic: units of observation, candidate variables, source hierarchy, quality checks, extraction workflow, and future quantitative outputs.

The Transparency Layer

The transparency dataset asks a narrower question: how national or quasi-national research integrity bodies publish case material, and in what format. It measures public visibility of case handling, not the overall quality of a national misconduct system.

How It Works

The project follows a staged extraction workflow. The current app already reflects these stages, while the protocol keeps the research method explicit.

Find sourcesCollect official national, institutional, funder, legal, and reporting sources.
Draft overviewSynthesize each country into a standard overview with system type, actors, document classes, signals, and next focus.
Extract to appConvert the overview into structured JavaScript records for map, list, detail, and dossier views.
Add transparencyAttach case-publication visibility data and public resource links where the transparency overview covers the country.
Deepen dossiersExpand selected countries with system maps, network layers, committee directories, source backbones, and case-file publication routes.

The latest extraction pass, recorded on 17 April 2026, added eleven newly supplied readable overview countries: Bosnia and Herzegovina, Bulgaria, Cyprus, Iceland, Kosovo, Montenegro, North Macedonia, Romania, Russia, Serbia, and Turkey.

North Macedonia and Russia were renamed to the standard Overview <Country>.docx pattern before extraction. Romania was extracted after its restored DOCX became readable.

Dossier Depth

Forty country records currently include mature expanded dossier structures and are explicitly labelled as deep dossiers. Albania, Andorra, Armenia, Azerbaijan, Georgia, Liechtenstein, Malta, Monaco and San Marino now have first expanded app dossiers, but remain outside the deep-dossier count until overview documents and fuller case-output sweeps are complete.

Transparency Layer

The transparency overview began with the EU-27 plus the United Kingdom, Switzerland, Norway, and Iceland, and the transparency dataset currently contains 35 scored jurisdictions after the Kosovo case-file pass. Current scoring includes 8 structured or stable archive/page systems, 9 fragmented, selective or mixed systems, 11 summary-based or institutional-statement systems, 6 low-publication or procedure-only systems, and 1 country with no national publication route identified.

Known Corpus Gaps

Albania, Andorra, Armenia, Azerbaijan, Georgia, Liechtenstein, Malta, Monaco and San Marino have first expanded app dossiers but no overview documents yet. The United Kingdom overview has now been added and reconciled into the app dossier. Vatican City has a local data folder but is not yet represented as a country profile in the app.

Short Briefing

A concise version for explaining the project to others.

The LERU map pilot is building a source-linked overview of research integrity systems across Europe. It collects country-level evidence on national bodies, institutional procedures, codes, funders, case-publication routes, and public reporting, then turns that material into a searchable web app with a map, country profiles, and deeper dossiers.

The project matters because research integrity governance is visible in uneven ways across countries. Some systems have national decision archives, some publish annual summaries, some rely on institutional processes, and some have limited public case information. The map makes these differences easier to compare without losing the source trail behind each judgement.

The current version already contains 49 country records, 40 extracted country overview documents, a 1413-entry source registry, a transparency layer covering 35 jurisdictions, and a committee-and-case-file workflow now completed for all 40 deep dossiers. Albania, Andorra, Armenia, Azerbaijan, Georgia, Liechtenstein, Malta, Monaco and San Marino have been expanded from starter notes or data-folder-only status into first app dossiers, while Vatican City remains the only data-folder-only representation gap. The United Kingdom, Germany, France and Portugal have completed the first four quality-consolidation pilots; the next phase is to apply that method across the remaining high-yield dossiers, finish missing formal overviews, and keep boundary regimes separate.
