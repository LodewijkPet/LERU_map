Officer routing

Where a concern should go first

Netherlands

  1. Start local. Concerns normally begin with a confidential adviser, institutional CWI or board route.
  2. Use LOWI after local handling. LOWI gives independent, non-binding second-line advice for affiliated institutions.
  3. Track publication owner separately. LOWI, UNL, VH, NWO or a local institution may publish the visible trace.

Belgium

  1. First identify the lane. Flanders, FWB and federal boundary routes should not be averaged into one national path.
  2. Flanders. Local CWI/CRI first, VCWI second advice after completed local handling.
  3. FWB. Local CIS/CEIS first, CSIS second advice where the institution recognizes it.

System variables

Side-by-side operating model

Officer question Netherlands Belgium
What is the system shape? Code-driven national norm with institutional first-line handling and LOWI second-line advice. Federal container with a Belgium-wide code, federal funder/boundary layer and two community integrity architectures.
Who handles first? Institutional CWI, confidential adviser and board route at universities, UMCs, institutes and other affiliated bodies. Local Flemish CWI/CRI or local FWB CIS/CEIS, depending on institution and community lane.
Where is second-line advice? LOWI for affiliated institutions after institutional handling. VCWI in Flanders; CSIS in the French-speaking/FWB lane.
Who publishes case traces? LOWI opinions, UNL university pages, VH hbo pages, NWO annual reports, and selected institutional reports or micro-publications. VCWI annual reports, KU Leuven annual reports and UAntwerpen CWI reports are the strongest public lanes; CSIS/FWB remains mainly route-visible.
How should funders be coded? NWO is a safety-net/annual-report route; ZonMw is grant-terms and compliance evidence. FWO points allegations to host institutions and VCWI context; FNRS connects integrity to CSIS, open science, DMPs and ethics/transparency; BELSPO sits in the federal programme and research-security layer.
What is the main comparison risk? Over-coding the country as LOWI only, ignoring UNL/VH/NWO and local publication owners. Flattening Belgium into one national board and losing the Flemish/FWB split.

Transparency lanes to extract next

Netherlands high-yield lanes

  • LOWI 2025-2026 opinions by institution, admissibility, boundary issue, finding and follow-up visibility.
  • UNL 2025-2026 rows by institution, topic, CWI advice, board decision, LOWI interaction and publication owner.
  • VH 2022 and 2025 hbo files against the VH publication-format rule.
  • NWO annual-report fields and local/non-UNL seeds: UvA, RIVM, Sanquin and Prinses Maxima.

Belgium high-yield lanes

  • VCWI annual reports 2013-2025 by second-advice request, report count, theme, outcome, follow-up and publication exclusion.
  • KU Leuven annual reports by notification count, admissibility, allegation category, outcome and anonymized-summary depth.
  • UAntwerpen annual reports by FFP/OUP class, outcome, formal follow-up, VCWI status and narrative-summary theme.
  • CSIS/FWB retesting for any public annual report, anonymized opinion, recommendation or output page.

Boundary controls

Keep these lanes separate

Human research and clinical trials

Netherlands: CCMO/MREC route. Belgium: FAMHP/ethics committees and CTIS. These are approval/regulatory routes unless a source links them to a misconduct finding.

Animal research

Netherlands: CCD/DEC/IvD/NVWA. Belgium: regional animal-experiment authorities and institutional committees. Code region and authority explicitly.

Security, data, IP and open science

Netherlands: knowledge security, data, privacy, social safety and IP boundaries. Belgium: BELSPO research security, DPA/GDPR, dual use, IP and open-science/DMP routes.

Officer checklist

Questions to answer before comparing cases

  • Is the issue core research integrity, or a boundary regime such as ethics approval, data, IP, employment or safety?
  • Which institution handled the first line, and which board or director made the final institutional decision?
  • Is the second-line body advisory, appeal-like, disciplinary, or only a general resource?
  • Who owns the public trace: the handling institution, a sector hub, a funder, or a second-line body?
  • Does the public record contain case-level facts, aggregate counts, procedure only, or no output?
  • Which source language, community lane, procedure version and access date need to travel with the extraction row?
